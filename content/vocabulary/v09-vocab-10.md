**大人** *(otona)*

"大人" refers to guests considered adults, typically over a set age like 12 or 13. Staff calculate rates, allocate beds, and prepare meals accordingly. Differentiating adults from children is important for billing and service. Accurate counting prevents disputes and ensures smooth check-in.

*Example: 大人2名で予約しております。* (The reservation is for 2 adults.)

---

## Quiz

**Guest asks: "How many adults are included in this booking?" Which category applies?**

- A. 大人 (adult)
- B. 子供 (child)
- C. 幼児 (infant)
- D. 人数 (number of people)

**Correct Answer:** 大人 (adult)

**Explanation:** 大人 (adult) determines the base room rate — Japanese hotels typically define 大人 as 12+ or 13+, but the threshold varies; always confirm the hotel's age policy before applying adult rates to avoid overcharging families.
