**遠くに** *(tooku ni)*

"遠くに" means far away. Staff use it to indicate locations requiring longer travel time. It is helpful for sightseeing, attractions, or distant facilities. Clear explanation avoids guest confusion and planning errors.

*Example: 山はホテルから遠くに見えます。* (The mountain can be seen far away from the hotel.)

---

## Quiz

**Guest asks: "Is the station close?" (It is actually far.) Best response?**

- A. 遠くにございます (it is far away)
- B. 近くにございます (it is nearby)
- C. 隣です (it is next door)
- D. 奥です (it is at the back)

**Correct Answer:** 遠くにございます (it is far away)

**Explanation:** 遠くにございます — honesty about distance is part of good service; follow it with a transport suggestion (バスや電車が便利です) so the information remains helpful rather than just discouraging.
