## 出勤 (しゅっきん · shukkin)

**Meaning:** going to work

出勤 refers to arriving at the workplace to start one's duties. Punctual attendance is crucial in maintaining operational efficiency. Proper recording of arrival times ensures accountability. Employees often check in at the start of each shift or workday.

**Example:**

毎朝9時に出勤します。

*I go to work at 9 AM every morning.*

---

## Quiz

**What is the Japanese term for arriving at work?**

- A. 退勤 (leaving work)
- B. 出勤 (going to work)
- C. 勤務 (work / duty)
- D. シフト (shift)

**Correct Answer:** 出勤 (going to work)

**Explanation:** 出勤 (shukkin) means going to work or arriving at work. 退勤 = leaving work, 勤務 = duty, シフト = shift schedule.
