**予約** *(yoyaku)*

"予約" means reservation and is central to hotel operations. Guests typically book rooms in advance, and staff must confirm these details accurately. It is often used together with polite questions. Miscommunication here can cause major issues.

---

## Quiz

**Which is the most polite way to ask about a reservation?**

- A. 予約ある？ (do you have a reservation?)
- B. 予約ありますか？ (do you have a reservation?)
- C. ご予約はございますか？ (do you have a reservation?)
- D. 予約ですか (is it a reservation?)

**Correct Answer:** ご予約はございますか？ (do you have a reservation?)

**Explanation:** ご予約はございますか？ uses the honorific prefix ご and the polite ございます — the standard greeting at any front desk.
