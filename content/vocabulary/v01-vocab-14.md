**恐れ入ります** *(osore irimasu)*

This phrase is used to politely say "excuse me" or "sorry to bother you." It shows humility and respect toward the listener. It is often used before making a request or asking a question. It is very common in customer service.

---

## Quiz

**____、もう一度お名前をお願いします。
(Excuse me, may I have your name again?)**

- A. 失礼いたします (excuse me)
- B. 申し訳ございません (I am very sorry)
- C. 恐れ入ります (I appreciate / excuse me (polite))
- D. 少々お待ちください (please wait a moment)

**Correct Answer:** 恐れ入ります (I appreciate / excuse me (polite))

**Explanation:** 恐れ入ります expresses humble regret for the inconvenience of a request — softer and more deferential than 失礼いたします.
