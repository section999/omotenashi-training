**お願いいたします** *(onegai itashimasu)*

This is a very polite way to say "please" when making a request. It is often used in formal situations like hotels or business settings. Compared to "onegai shimasu," it is more respectful. It is commonly used when asking guests to do something.

---

## Quiz

**こちらにご記入を____。
(Please fill this out.)**

- A. かしこまりました (certainly / understood (very formal))
- B. ありがとうございます (thank you)
- C. 恐れ入ります (I appreciate / excuse me (polite))
- D. お願いいたします (please / I humbly request)

**Correct Answer:** お願いいたします (please / I humbly request)

**Explanation:** お願いいたします is a very polite form of "please" — more respectful than onegai shimasu, suitable for all formal requests.
