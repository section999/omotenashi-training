**向かい** *(mukai)*

"向かい" indicates a location directly across from another. It is essential for describing streets, parking, or rooms. Staff should clearly identify the reference point. Proper use prevents guests from walking in the wrong direction.

*Example: 郵便局は駅の向かいにございます。* (The post office is opposite the station.)

---

## Quiz

**Guest asks: "Where is the pharmacy?"**

- A. 駅の向かいです (it is across from the station)
- B. 隣です (it is next door)
- C. 奥です (it is at the back)
- D. 手前です (it is in front)

**Correct Answer:** 駅の向かいです (it is across from the station)

**Explanation:** 向かい (directly opposite) — always name the reference point (駅の向かい, ホテルの向かい) so guests know what to look across from; without the reference, 向かい is ambiguous.
