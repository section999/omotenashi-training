**エレベーター** *(erebeetaa)*

"エレベーター" refers to a lift used to move guests between floors in a hotel or building. It is one of the most essential facilities, especially for guests with luggage, elderly guests, or those with mobility issues. Staff should clearly explain its location, usage, and safety precautions, including emergency procedures.

In busy hotels, guiding guests to the correct elevator and informing them about keycard access or floor restrictions is also important for security and smooth operation.

---

## Quiz

**A guest asks: "How do I get to the 10th floor?"**

- A. エレベーターで10階までお上がりください (please take the elevator to the 10th floor)
- B. 階段で行ってください (please take the stairs)
- C. 分かりません (I don't understand)
- D. 自分で探してください (please find it yourself)

**Correct Answer:** エレベーターで10階までお上がりください (please take the elevator to the 10th floor)

**Explanation:** エレベーターで10階までお上がりください — で marks the means of travel; お上がりください is the polite imperative of 上がる (to go up), more refined than ご利用ください in this directional context.
