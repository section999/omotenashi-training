title: Approaching the Guest
---

Every approach to a guest is a micro-interaction that sets a tone. Before you reach them, consider: what is their current state? Are they relaxed and settled, or scanning for something? Are they mid-conversation, or alone and open? Your posture, pace, and expression should be calibrated to the answer before you arrive at their side.

The approach should be **unhurried but purposeful**. Rushing signals anxiety; meandering signals indifference. A slight bow as you arrive acknowledges their presence before you speak. Your first words should be open and warm — never transactional. "Good evening, is everything to your liking?" is a different kind of opening than "Are you ready to order?"

---

## Quiz

**Why is the timing of approaching a guest so important?**

**Model Answer:** Approaching too early feels intrusive; too late signals neglect. The right moment — identified through observation — makes the guest feel genuinely cared for.

**Explanation:** Timing is the difference between service that feels natural and service that feels awkward.
