title: Service Begins Before the Guest Arrives
---

The most impressive hospitality often happens before any guest interaction takes place. The room that is perfectly prepared, the table that reflects a remembered preference, the lobby that feels welcoming the moment you walk in — these are the result of skilled, attentive preparation that the guest will never see.

**Kikubari (気配り)** — anticipatory attentiveness — begins at the preparation stage. Before a guest's arrival, review what you know about them: previous preferences, dietary requirements, the purpose of their visit. Then act on that knowledge without being asked. This is what distinguishes competent service from memorable hospitality.

---

## Quiz

**Service skills are only visible during direct guest interaction.**

**Answer:** False

**Explanation:** Many service skills occur behind the scenes — especially during preparation before the guest arrives.
