title: The Language of Respect — Keigo
---

Japanese hospitality is inseparable from its language. **Keigo (敬語)** is the system of honorific speech used in all professional guest-facing contexts. It has three registers:

| Type | Japanese | Purpose | Example |
|------|----------|---------|---------|
| Sonkeigo | 尊敬語 | Elevates the guest's actions | いらっしゃいます (to be/come) |
| Kenjōgo | 謙譲語 | Humbles the speaker's own actions | 参ります (I will go) |
| Teineigo | 丁寧語 | General polite speech | です / ます forms |

Choosing the correct register is not merely grammar — it is an act of respect. Using *sonkeigo* to describe the guest's actions elevates them; using *kenjōgo* for your own actions demonstrates humility. Together, they create the respectful distance that underpins Japanese hospitality.

## Quiz

**Which Keigo type elevates the customer's own action?**

- A. Teineigo
- B. Kenjōgo
- C. Sonkeigo
- D. Tamego

**Correct Answer:** Sonkeigo

**Explanation:** Sonkeigo (尊敬語) is used to elevate the guest's actions, e.g. いらっしゃいます.
