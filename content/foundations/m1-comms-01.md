title: Reading the Air
---

**空気を読む (kuuki wo yomu)** — literally "reading the air" — is one of the most important social skills in Japanese culture, and it is indispensable in hospitality. It means sensing the unspoken mood of a situation and adjusting your behaviour accordingly, without being told what is needed.

A guest who responds to your greeting in a short, clipped tone may be in a hurry — match their pace, be efficient, do not prolong the interaction. A guest who lingers at check-in and asks questions about the area is open to conversation — engage warmly and provide more than the minimum. Neither guest told you what they needed; you read it.

This skill cannot be scripted. It is developed through observation, experience, and genuine interest in the person in front of you.

## Quiz

**What does 'reading the air' (空気を読む) mean in a hospitality context?**

- A. Checking the weather forecast
- B. Following a fixed script
- C. Sensing a guest's unspoken mood and adjusting behaviour
- D. Announcing your arrival clearly

**Correct Answer:** Sensing a guest's unspoken mood and adjusting behaviour

**Explanation:** It refers to sensing the guest's mood and adjusting behaviour accordingly — without being asked.
