title: Historical Roots — The Tea Ceremony
---

The origins of omotenashi trace back to the Japanese **tea ceremony (茶道 / sadō)**. In the tea ceremony, the host prepares every detail before the guest arrives — the hanging scroll, the seasonal flowers, the temperature of the water, the placement of every utensil. Nothing is improvised; everything communicates: *you are welcome here, and I have thought deeply about your comfort.*

**Wabi-sabi (侘び寂び)** — the Japanese aesthetic of finding beauty in imperfection, transience, and simplicity — is deeply embedded in this tradition. The worn edge of a tea bowl, the asymmetry of a hand-thrown cup, the silence between words — these are not flaws but expressions of refinement. In hospitality, this translates into an appreciation for subtlety over spectacle: the understated, the seasonal, the quietly thoughtful.

## Quiz

**Which concept emphasises 'finding beauty in simplicity and imperfection'?**

- A. Kaizen
- B. Wabi-Sabi
- C. Zen
- D. Ikigai

**Correct Answer:** Wabi-Sabi

**Explanation:** Wabi-Sabi values subtlety, naturalness, and understated elegance, often reflected in décor and atmosphere.
