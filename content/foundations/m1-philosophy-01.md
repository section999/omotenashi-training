title: What is Omotenashi?
---

**Omotenashi (おもてなし)** is the Japanese philosophy of wholehearted hospitality. Unlike transactional service, omotenashi is rooted in genuine care for the guest — anticipating their needs before they are expressed, and serving without any expectation of reward or recognition in return.

The word itself contains two layers of meaning: *omote* (表) meaning "to show outwardly," and *nashi* (なし) meaning "nothing hidden." True omotenashi, therefore, is service with complete transparency of intention — nothing concealed, nothing performed for show. It is care made visible through action.

The highest form of omotenashi is when the guest feels completely at ease without being aware of the effort behind it. The host's preparation, attention, and care are felt rather than seen.

## Quiz

**What is the core principle of Japanese hospitality?**

- A. Offering discounts to repeat guests
- B. Omotenashi — anticipating guest needs before they ask
- C. Following a strict service manual
- D. Always providing western-style service

**Correct Answer:** Omotenashi — anticipating guest needs before they ask

**Explanation:** Omotenashi emphasizes proactive care and anticipating needs, rather than reacting to requests.
