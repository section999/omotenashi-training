title: Pacing and Timing
---

Not all service should be delivered at the same speed. A guest checking in late at night after a long flight wants efficiency — clear, quick, warm, done. A guest dining for a special occasion wants to feel that the evening is unhurried, that no one is waiting for them to finish. **Faster is not always better.**

Read the pace of each interaction and match it. In F&B, this means reading the table — if conversation is flowing and plates are barely touched, do not clear; if the meal is clearly finished and the guest is checking their watch, be present and ready. The art of pacing is making the guest feel that the experience moves at their rhythm, not yours.

---

## Quiz

**Faster service is always better service.**

**Answer:** False

**Explanation:** Accuracy, attention, and timing matter more than speed. Service should match the guest's rhythm.
