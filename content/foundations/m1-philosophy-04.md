title: Proactive vs Reactive Service
---

The distinction at the heart of omotenashi is the difference between **reactive** and **proactive** service. Reactive service responds to what the guest asks for. Proactive service anticipates what the guest will need — before they ask, or before they even realise they need it.

A guest who has been travelling for six hours may not think to ask for a hot towel or a warmer room temperature. An omotenashi-minded host will have already prepared these. The result: the guest feels not merely served, but *understood*. This is the emotional core of omotenashi — to make the guest feel that someone has genuinely thought about them as a person.

This proactive orientation requires constant, attentive observation. You cannot anticipate what you have not noticed. The skill of **kikubari (気配り)** — reading subtle cues and acting on them — is the practical expression of this philosophy.

---

## Quiz

**Omotenashi is only about performing polite actions when a guest requests something.**

**Answer:** False

**Explanation:** Omotenashi is proactive — it means anticipating needs before a guest asks. Waiting to be asked is the opposite of its spirit.
