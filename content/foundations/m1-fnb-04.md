title: The Art of Recommendation
---

In Japanese hospitality, a recommendation is an act of care — not a sales tool. When a staff member suggests a dish or a pairing, it should feel like the advice of someone who genuinely knows the menu and wants the guest to have the best possible experience. It should never feel like an upsell.

To recommend effectively: know every dish on the menu personally, including its flavour profile, its ingredients, and its appropriate pairing. Know which dishes are currently at their seasonal best. And — critically — pay attention to what the guest has already ordered or expressed interest in. A recommendation that follows from what the guest clearly enjoys feels like a gift; one that seems random or self-serving feels like a sales pitch.

## Quiz

**In Japanese hospitality, upselling is primarily about increasing revenue.**

**Answer:** False

**Explanation:** Effective upselling in this context is an act of care — offering enhancements the guest would genuinely appreciate.
