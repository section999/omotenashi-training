title: When to Escalate
---

Not every complaint should be escalated. Escalating unnecessarily makes the guest wait and signals that the front-line team lacks authority or competence. Escalate when:

- The issue involves **health, safety, or legal liability**
- Financial compensation or a significant upgrade is required
- The guest specifically requests to speak with a manager
- The situation is beyond your authority to resolve

When escalating, **brief the manager before the guest repeats themselves**. The guest should never have to explain the situation twice. Hand over with: the guest's name, the nature of the complaint, what has already been offered, and any relevant background. A well-briefed escalation demonstrates professionalism; a poorly briefed one compounds the original failure.

## Quiz

**When should you escalate a guest complaint to a manager?**

- A. Always, for every complaint
- B. Only when the guest demands it
- C. For health/safety issues, financial disputes, or matters beyond your authority
- D. After the guest has left

**Correct Answer:** For health/safety issues, financial disputes, or matters beyond your authority

**Explanation:** Escalation should be swift and well-briefed — the guest should never have to repeat themselves to the manager.
