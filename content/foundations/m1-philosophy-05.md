title: Wabi-Sabi in the Modern Hotel
---

Wabi-sabi is sometimes misunderstood as a preference for the rustic or the austere. In practice, it guides the aesthetic sensibility of Japanese hospitality toward **restraint, seasonality, and the absence of the unnecessary**. A room decorated with a single seasonal flower arrangement and natural materials communicates more care than a room overfilled with lavish decorations.

In the modern luxury hotel context, wabi-sabi appears in: the use of local, seasonal ingredients in kaiseki cuisine; natural materials — wood, stone, linen — in room design; the preference for calm and quiet over entertainment; and the considered absence of clutter. The message is always the same: *we have thought about what is truly needed, and nothing more.*

## Quiz

**Wabi-Sabi encourages lavish decoration to impress guests.**

**Answer:** False

**Explanation:** Wabi-Sabi values simplicity, natural materials, and understated elegance — not lavish decoration.
