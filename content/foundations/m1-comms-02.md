title: The Meaning of Eye Contact
---

In Western contexts, sustained eye contact signals confidence and engagement. In Japanese hospitality, the approach is more nuanced. Eye contact should be **brief, warm, and intentional** — enough to signal genuine attention, not so prolonged that it creates discomfort or feels confrontational.

When bowing, eye contact is naturally broken — this is appropriate and respectful. When receiving a complaint or apology, a downward gaze communicates humility and acknowledgement. When guiding a guest to their table, periodic glances confirm that they are following comfortably. Eye contact, like all communication, must be responsive to the moment.

## Quiz

**What is the correct approach to eye contact in Japanese service?**

- A. Maintain constant direct eye contact
- B. Avoid eye contact entirely
- C. Brief, warm, and intentional — not sustained or piercing
- D. Only make eye contact when spoken to

**Correct Answer:** Brief, warm, and intentional — not sustained or piercing

**Explanation:** Proper eye contact builds trust while maintaining the guest's comfort.
