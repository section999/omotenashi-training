おもてなし

O · MO · TE · NA · SHI

Derived from the phrase omote (surface) and nashi (without)
— to serve from the heart, concealing no hidden agenda.
The word itself encodes the philosophy:
pure, transparent, genuine hospitality offered with the whole self.

the japanese way

This course instills four foundational pillars that distinguish Japanese hospitality from service anywhere else in the world.

  • 🎯

    Anticipation (先読み)

    Respond to needs before they are voiced. True service means reading context, not just requests.

  • 🌿

    Selflessness (無償の心)

    Service given without expectation of reward or recognition. The guest's experience is the only measure.

  • ⚡

    Perfection (完璧主義)

    Attention to every detail — posture, tone, timing, environment — executed with quiet precision.

  • 🤝

    Sincerity (誠実さ)

    Warmth that is genuine, not performed. The guest should feel valued as a person, not a transaction.

learning outcomes

By the end of this course, you'll have the language, mindset, and practical skills to work confidently.

1

Greet and serve guests using proper Keigo and formal Japanese service language across all interactions.

2

Handle complaints professionally using the LEARN protocol and Japanese apology etiquette.

3

Communicate with colleagues using Hou-Ren-Sou reporting and understand Senpai-Kouhai workplace dynamics.
