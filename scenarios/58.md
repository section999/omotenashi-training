## Turn 1

**Guest:** I'd like to use the hot spring. Are there any rules I should know about?
**Guest JP:** 温泉を利用したいのですが、何かルールはありますか？

### Choices

- [2] ありがとうございます。いくつかお願いがございます。入浴前にシャワーで体をお流しいただき、タオルを湯船に入れないようにお願いいたします。また、浴衣またはお持ちのタオルをお持ちの上、ご利用くださいませ。
  - EN: Thank you for asking. There are a few guidelines. Please rinse your body in the shower before entering the bath, and please keep your towel out of the water. Please also bring your yukata or your own towel when you come.
  - Feedback: Perfect. Covers the key onsen etiquette points warmly and proactively, setting the guest up for a great experience.
- [1] シャワーをしてから入ってください。タオルは湯船に入れないでください。
  - EN: Please shower before entering. Don't put your towel in the bath.
  - Feedback: Correct rules but the tone is instructional rather than welcoming. A warm framing improves the guest experience.
- [0] 特にルールはないですよ。自由にどうぞ。
  - EN: There are no particular rules. Feel free.
  - Feedback: Incorrect. Onsen etiquette is important in Japanese culture and must be communicated.
- [0] ルールはたくさんあるのでフロントに来てください。
  - EN: There are many rules so please come to the front desk.
  - Feedback: Unnecessarily vague if the guest is on the phone or already at the desk. Explain the key rules directly.

## Turn 2

**System:** The guest thanks you for the explanation but has a specific concern.

**Guest:** I actually have a small tattoo on my wrist. Is that allowed?
**Guest JP:** 実は手首に小さなタトゥーがあるのですが、大丈夫でしょうか？

### Choices

- [2] ご質問いただきありがとうございます。当ホテルの温泉では、タトゥーのある方にも安心してご利用いただけるよう、個室風呂をご用意しております。よろしければご案内いたします。
  - EN: Thank you for asking. At our hotel's hot spring, we have a private bath available so guests with tattoos can also enjoy the facilities comfortably. We would be happy to show you to it if you wish.
  - Feedback: Excellent. Addresses a sensitive topic with grace, offers a practical solution, and maintains a welcoming tone throughout.
- [1] タトゥーがある方は通常の温泉はご利用いただけませんが、個室風呂があります。
  - EN: Guests with tattoos cannot use the general hot spring, but we have a private bath.
  - Feedback: Accurate but the delivery is blunt. Leading with the restriction before the solution can feel unwelcoming.
- [0] タトゥーがある方は入れません。
  - EN: Guests with tattoos cannot enter.
  - Feedback: While this may be the policy for the shared bath, it ignores alternative options and leaves the guest with no recourse.
- [0] 小さいから大丈夫じゃないですか？
  - EN: It's small so it's probably fine.
  - Feedback: Casual and uncertain. Do not give unofficial exceptions to policy without proper authority.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guest decides to use the private bath and asks one more question.

**Guest:** What time does the onsen close?
**Guest JP:** 温泉は何時まで利用できますか？

### Choices

- [2] 大浴場は午前11時から午後11時まで、個室風呂は24時間いつでもご利用いただけます。最終受付は閉館の30分前でございます。
  - EN: The large communal bath is open from 11 AM to 11 PM, and the private baths are available 24 hours. Last admission is 30 minutes before closing.
  - Feedback: Perfect. Provides hours for both options relevant to the guest, and includes the important last-admission detail.
- [1] 午後11時までです。
  - EN: Until 11 PM.
  - Feedback: Correct for the shared bath, but since the guest will use the private bath, their specific hours should be clarified.
- [0] 早めに行ったほうがいいですよ。
  - EN: You should go early.
  - Feedback: Does not answer the question. State the specific closing time.
- [0] 閉まる時間は変わることがあります。
  - EN: The closing time can change sometimes.
  - Feedback: Unhelpful vagueness. Provide standard hours and note exceptions only if relevant.
