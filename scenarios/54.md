---
id: 54
title: Luggage Storage
description: A guest who has checked out wants to store their bags while they go sightseeing.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** We've just checked out but we'd like to leave our bags here while we look around the city. Is that possible?
**Guest JP:** チェックアウトしたのですが、観光している間、荷物を預けることはできますか？

### Choices

- [2] もちろんでございます。チェックアウト後もお荷物をお預かりするサービスをご用意しております。喜んでお預かりいたします。
  - EN: Of course. We offer a luggage storage service even after check-out. We would be happy to look after your bags.
  - Feedback: Perfect. Confirms the service exists, uses polite language, and offers enthusiastically.
- [1] はい、預かれます。こちらにどうぞ。
  - EN: Yes, we can store them. Please come this way.
  - Feedback: Helpful but brief. Adding some welcoming language improves the guest experience.
- [0] チェックアウト後は荷物は預かれません。
  - EN: We cannot store luggage after check-out.
  - Feedback: Incorrect and unhelpful. Most hotels offer this service; always verify before refusing.
- [0] ロビーに置いておいてください。
  - EN: Just leave them in the lobby.
  - Feedback: Irresponsible. Unattended bags in the lobby are a security concern.

## Turn 2

**System:** You begin the check-in process for luggage storage and the guest asks a follow-up question.

**Guest:** How many bags can we store? We have four suitcases and two backpacks.
**Guest JP:** いくつまで預けられますか？スーツケースが4つとバックパックが2つあります。

### Choices

- [2] 6点すべてお預かりできます。お荷物にこちらの番号札をお付けしますので、お引き取りの際にお持ちくださいませ。
  - EN: We can store all six pieces. We will attach a numbered tag to each bag — please keep the tags for collection.
  - Feedback: Excellent. Answers directly, reassures the guest all bags are accepted, and explains the tag system clearly.
- [1] 全部預かれます。番号札をお渡しします。
  - EN: We can store all of them. I'll give you a numbered tag.
  - Feedback: Correct but lacks a clear instruction about bringing the tags back, which is important for retrieval.
- [0] 決まった数はありません。置く場所があれば大丈夫です。
  - EN: There's no set number. As long as there's space it's fine.
  - Feedback: Vague and unprofessional. Give a clear and confident answer.
- [0] そんなにたくさんはちょっと…。
  - EN: That many might be a bit...
  - Feedback: Hesitant and unhelpful. Check storage capacity and give a definitive answer.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guest has been sightseeing all day and returns to the hotel to collect their bags in the evening.

**Guest:** Hi, we're back to pick up our bags. Here are our tags.
**Guest JP:** ただいま戻りました。荷物を受け取りに来ました。こちらが番号札です。

### Choices

- [2] おかえりなさいませ。番号札をありがとうございます。ただいまお荷物をお持ちいたします。観光はいかがでしたか？
  - EN: Welcome back. Thank you for the tags. I will bring your bags right now. How was your sightseeing?
  - Feedback: Perfect. おかえりなさいませ is the ideal welcome-back phrase, the bags are retrieved promptly, and the warm question shows genuine hospitality.
- [1] 番号札を確認します。少し待ってください。
  - EN: I'll check the tags. Please wait a moment.
  - Feedback: Functional but misses the welcome-back greeting which is an important hospitality touch.
- [0] 荷物はあそこにあります。自分で取ってきてください。
  - EN: Your bags are over there. Please get them yourself.
  - Feedback: Rude and counter to hotel service standards. Always retrieve bags for the guest.
- [0] 番号札がないと渡せません。
  - EN: I can't hand them over without the tags.
  - Feedback: While tags are important, the guest provided them. This response ignores what they said.
