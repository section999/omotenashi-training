---
id: 70
title: Bill Dispute at Check-out
description: Guest at check-out questions a charge they do not recognise on their bill.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Excuse me, there's a 3,000 yen charge on my bill that I don't recognise. What is it for?
**Guest JP:** すみません、請求書に3,000円の請求があるんですが、何の費用かわかりません。

### Choices

- [2] ご不便をおかけして申し訳ございません。ただいまシステムを確認いたしますので、少々お待ちくださいませ。内容がわかり次第、詳しくご説明いたします。
  - EN: I am sorry for any confusion. I will check the system right now — please bear with me for just a moment. As soon as I have the details, I will explain everything clearly.
  - Feedback: Apologises promptly, commits to a quick check, and promises a full explanation — the ideal first response to a billing concern.
- [1] 確認いたします。少々お待ちください。
  - EN: I will check. Please wait a moment.
  - Feedback: Efficient and professional, but lacks a brief apology for the confusion and gives no indication of how long the check will take.
- [0] お客様が使われたものですよ。
  - EN: It is something you used.
  - Feedback: Assuming the charge is correct and implying the guest is wrong — before even verifying — is dismissive and likely to escalate the situation.
- [0] 領収書はお部屋に置いてあるはずです。
  - EN: The receipt should be in your room.
  - Feedback: Directing the guest to find their own documentation at check-out is unhelpful — the front desk should resolve this directly.

## Turn 2

**System:** You check the system and find that the 3,000 yen charge is for items taken from the minibar.

**Guest:** (Waiting for your explanation)
**Guest JP:** （説明を待っています）

### Choices

- [2] お待たせいたしました。確認いたしましたところ、こちらの3,000円はミニバーのご利用料金でございます。明細によりますと、〇〇日にミニバーより〇〇をご利用いただいた記録がございます。こちらの明細書をご覧いただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: Thank you for waiting. I have checked and this 3,000 yen charge is for minibar usage. According to the itemised record, there is a log of minibar items used on the Xth. Would you like to take a look at the itemised breakdown?
  - Feedback: Thanks the guest for waiting, identifies the charge clearly, provides the specific evidence, and invites the guest to verify — calm, thorough, and professional.
- [1] 3,000円はミニバーのご利用料金です。ご確認いただけますか？
  - EN: The 3,000 yen is a minibar charge. Would you like to confirm it?
  - Feedback: Clear and factual, but does not show the itemised record or explain the date, which may not fully resolve the guest's uncertainty.
- [0] ミニバーを使っていませんか？
  - EN: Did you not use the minibar?
  - Feedback: Questioning the guest in this way sounds accusatory — present the facts calmly rather than putting the guest on the defensive.
- [0] ミニバーは自動でチェックされています。
  - EN: The minibar is checked automatically.
  - Feedback: Citing the system as authority without explaining what was charged does nothing to clarify the guest's question.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guest reviews the itemised record and accepts the explanation.

**Guest:** OK, I understand. Can I pay by credit card? And could you send the receipt to my email?
**Guest JP:** わかりました。クレジットカードで払えますか？あと、領収書をメールで送ってもらえますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。クレジットカードでのお支払いはご利用いただけます。またメールでの領収書の送付も承りますので、ご登録のメールアドレスをご確認させていただいてもよろしいでしょうか。本日はご不便をおかけし、誠に申し訳ございませんでした。またのお越しを心よりお待ち申し上げております。
  - EN: Certainly. We do accept credit card payments. We can also send the receipt by email — may I confirm the email address we have on file for you? We sincerely apologise for any confusion today. We look forward to welcoming you back again.
  - Feedback: Confirms both requests, asks for the email efficiently, apologises once more for the confusion, and closes with a warm farewell — a complete and gracious check-out.
- [1] はい、クレジットカードで大丈夫です。メールアドレスを教えていただけますか？
  - EN: Yes, credit card is fine. Could you give me your email address?
  - Feedback: Handles both requests correctly but misses a final apology for the billing confusion and lacks a warm send-off.
- [0] メール送信は時間がかかる場合があります。
  - EN: Sending by email may take some time.
  - Feedback: Leading with a caveat rather than simply accepting the request makes the service feel reluctant and unresponsive.
- [0] 紙の領収書でよくないですか？
  - EN: Isn't a paper receipt fine?
  - Feedback: Questioning the guest's stated preference rather than simply accommodating it is unwelcoming, especially at the close of an already sensitive interaction.
