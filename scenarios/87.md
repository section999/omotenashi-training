title: Inappropriate Guest Behaviour Toward Staff
description: The duty manager must handle a guest who made inappropriate comments to a female staff member, firmly but diplomatically.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** (Door opens) Yes?
**Guest JP:** （ドアが開く）はい？

### Choices

- [2] 突然お邪魔して失礼いたします。私はデューティーマネージャーの○○と申します。本日のスタッフとのやり取りについて、少しお時間をいただきたいのですが、よろしいでしょうか。お部屋の外でお話しできますと幸いです。
  - EN: I apologise for disturbing you. My name is [Name] and I am the duty manager. I would like to speak with you briefly regarding an interaction that took place with one of our staff members today. Could I take just a moment of your time? It would be helpful to speak outside your room.
  - Feedback: Introduces themselves formally, states the purpose without accusation, requests privacy respectfully, and maintains a professional but non-threatening tone — the correct approach to a sensitive conduct matter.
- [1] デューティーマネージャーの○○です。本日スタッフの件についてお話しできますか？
  - EN: I am the duty manager. Can I speak to you about a staff matter today?
  - Feedback: States the role and purpose, but is vague about the nature of the conversation and does not request a private setting.
- [0] 本日、スタッフに対して不適切な発言をされましたね。
  - EN: You made inappropriate comments to our staff today.
  - Feedback: Opening with a direct accusation before hearing any response will immediately put the guest on the defensive and close down productive dialogue.
- [0] スタッフからクレームが入っています。
  - EN: A complaint has been filed against you by a staff member.
  - Feedback: Framing this as a formal complaint filing before any conversation has taken place is unnecessarily confrontational for a first approach.

## Turn 2

**System:** The guest denies the incident; you must maintain composure and explain the hotel's conduct policy without accusation.

**Guest:** I don't know what you're talking about. I didn't do anything wrong.
**Guest JP:** 何のことかわかりません。何も悪いことはしていません。

### Choices

- [2] ご意見を伺いまして、ありがとうございます。何かご誤解があった可能性もございますが、当ホテルではスタッフとお客様の双方が安心してご滞在・勤務できる環境を大切にしております。特定の方を非難するためではなく、その方針をお伝えするためにお声がけいたしました。今後もどうか快適にお過ごしいただけますよう願っております。
  - EN: Thank you for sharing your perspective. It is possible there has been a misunderstanding. I want to assure you that our hotel is committed to providing a respectful and comfortable environment for both guests and staff. I came to speak with you not to accuse anyone, but simply to share our commitment to that standard. I hope the rest of your stay is an enjoyable one.
  - Feedback: Acknowledges the denial without dismissing it, explains the policy calmly without accusation, and keeps the door open for a positive conclusion — this is the model for de-escalating a conduct matter diplomatically.
- [1] 誤解でしたら大変失礼いたしました。ただ、当ホテルではスタッフへのご配慮をお願いしております。
  - EN: If it was a misunderstanding, I apologise. However, we do ask guests to treat staff respectfully.
  - Feedback: Handles the denial reasonably and states the expectation, but the phrasing could be misread as implying the guest is still suspected — needs a little more diplomatic framing.
- [0] スタッフはそのようなことを言わないと思います。
  - EN: I do not believe our staff would say something like that.
  - Feedback: Contradicting the guest's denial by implying the staff member's account must be true is directly confrontational and will escalate the situation.
- [0] 今後このようなことが続く場合はご退去いただく場合があります。
  - EN: If this continues, you may be asked to leave the hotel.
  - Feedback: Threatening eviction when the guest has denied the incident and no further behaviour has occurred is a disproportionate escalation that may create a more serious incident.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guest apologises and the situation de-escalates; you thank them for their understanding and close the conversation professionally.

**Guest:** Look, maybe I did come across the wrong way. I'll be more careful. I apologise.
**Guest JP:** もしかしたら、誤解を与えてしまったかもしれません。気をつけます。申し訳ありませんでした。

### Choices

- [2] ご理解いただきありがとうございます。そのようにおっしゃっていただき、大変有難く存じます。当ホテルスタッフも、お客様に心地よくお過ごしいただけるよう全力を尽くします。引き続き素晴らしいご滞在をお楽しみいただけますよう心よりお祈り申し上げます。
  - EN: Thank you very much for your understanding — that is greatly appreciated. Our team will continue to do everything they can to make your stay as enjoyable as possible. I sincerely hope you have a wonderful rest of your time with us.
  - Feedback: Accepts the apology graciously without dwelling on the incident, reaffirms the hotel's service commitment, and closes on a genuinely warm note — a model de-escalation conclusion.
- [1] ありがとうございます。これからも快適にご滞在ください。
  - EN: Thank you. Please continue to enjoy your stay.
  - Feedback: Closes the conversation adequately, but does not express the warmth or reaffirm the hotel's commitment to the guest's experience that a full resolution deserves.
- [0] 今後は気をつけていただけると助かります。
  - EN: It would be helpful if you could be careful going forward.
  - Feedback: Repeating the instruction to be careful after the guest has already apologised is unnecessary and slightly condescending — trust the resolution.
- [0] スタッフに対してもお詫びいただければと思います。
  - EN: I would appreciate it if you could also apologise to the staff member.
  - Feedback: Demanding an apology to the staff member at this stage, when the situation has been resolved through management, is likely to re-escalate the matter.
