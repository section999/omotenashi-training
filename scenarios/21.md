## Turn 1

**Guest:** Do you offer currency exchange here?
**Guest JP:** こちらで両替はできますか？

### Choices

- [2] はい、フロントにて両替サービスをご提供しております。ご希望の通貨はございますか？
  - EN: Yes, we offer a currency exchange service at the front desk. What currency would you like?
  - Feedback: Confirms service and asks for the currency.
- [1] はい、できます。
  - EN: Yes, we can.
  - Feedback: Confirms but doesn't progress the interaction.
- [0] 銀行に行ってください。
  - EN: Please go to a bank.
  - Feedback: If the hotel offers it, don't deflect.
- [0] ドルだけです。
  - EN: We only have dollars.
  - Feedback: Starts with a restriction before asking what's needed.

## Turn 2

**System:** You confirm the currency exchange service is available and ask what the guest needs.

**Guest:** I'd like to exchange 200 USD to yen.
**Guest JP:** 200ドルを円に両替したいのですが。

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。本日のレートは1ドル143円でございます。200ドルで28,600円のご両替となります。
  - EN: Certainly. Today's exchange rate is 143 yen per dollar. For 200 dollars, that would be 28,600 yen.
  - Feedback: Shows the rate and total clearly.
- [1] 少々お待ちください。確認します。
  - EN: Please wait a moment. I will check.
  - Feedback: Responsible but should have the rate available.
- [0] レートは悪いですよ。
  - EN: The rate is not very good.
  - Feedback: Unsolicited judgment. State the rate factually.
- [0] 銀行の方が得です。
  - EN: You would get a better deal at a bank.
  - Feedback: Self-defeating. Provide the service as offered.

## Turn 3

**System:** You look up the current exchange rate and calculate the amount for the guest.

**Guest:** That's fine. Please go ahead.
**Guest JP:** それで結構です。お願いします。

### Choices

- [2] ありがとうございます。28,600円、こちらでございます。ご確認ください。
  - EN: Thank you. Here is 28,600 yen. Please verify the amount.
  - Feedback: Presents money politely and asks for verification.
- [1] はい、どうぞ。
  - EN: Yes, here you are.
  - Feedback: Hands over but doesn't ask guest to verify.
- [0] 数えてください。
  - EN: Please count it.
  - Feedback: Too blunt. Politely present first.
- [0] 領収書いりますか？
  - EN: Do you need a receipt?
  - Feedback: Skips handing over the money first.
