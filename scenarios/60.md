---
id: 60
title: Early Check-in Request
description: A guest arrives at 9 AM and asks if they can check in before the standard time.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hi, I just arrived. I know it's early, but is it possible to check in now?
**Guest JP:** こんにちは、今到着しました。早いのはわかっているのですが、今すぐチェックインできますか？

### Choices

- [2] いらっしゃいませ。ご到着いただきありがとうございます。通常のチェックインは午後3時からでございますが、ただいまお部屋の状況を確認いたします。少々お待ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Welcome. Thank you for arriving. Standard check-in begins at 3 PM, but let me check the current status of your room right away. One moment please.
  - Feedback: Perfect. Welcomes the guest warmly, states policy clearly, and commits to checking rather than refusing outright.
- [1] チェックインは3時からですが、確認してみます。
  - EN: Check-in is from 3 PM, but I'll try to check.
  - Feedback: Acceptable, but lacks a welcoming greeting for a newly arrived guest and the phrasing is slightly casual.
- [0] チェックインは3時です。それまで待ってください。
  - EN: Check-in is at 3 PM. Please wait until then.
  - Feedback: Blunt and unhelpful. Always check availability and offer alternatives.
- [0] 早すぎます。またあとで来てください。
  - EN: It's too early. Please come back later.
  - Feedback: Dismissive of a guest who has just arrived. Offer solutions, not rejections.

## Turn 2

**System:** You check and the room is not ready yet. You offer alternatives while the guest waits.

**Guest:** Oh, I see. Is there anywhere I can wait comfortably?
**Guest JP:** そうですか。どこかゆっくり待てる場所はありますか？

### Choices

- [2] 申し訳ございません。お部屋の準備ができましたら、すぐにご連絡いたします。その間、お荷物はお預かりいたしますので、2階のラウンジにてごゆっくりおくつろぎくださいませ。お飲み物もご用意しております。
  - EN: We are so sorry for the wait. We will contact you as soon as your room is ready. In the meantime, we will look after your luggage, and please make yourself comfortable in the lounge on the second floor. Drinks are available for you there.
  - Feedback: Excellent. Apologizes, offers luggage storage, directs to the lounge, and mentions drinks — a full and considerate response that turns a disappointment into a positive experience.
- [1] ラウンジで待てます。荷物は預かります。
  - EN: You can wait in the lounge. We'll look after your bags.
  - Feedback: Helpful but lacks the apology for the inconvenience and does not mention any amenities in the lounge.
- [0] ロビーのソファーに座ってください。
  - EN: Please sit on the sofa in the lobby.
  - Feedback: Minimal effort. Does not offer luggage storage, lounge access, or any refreshments.
- [0] 部屋が準備できるまで外で時間をつぶしてください。
  - EN: Please find something to do outside until the room is ready.
  - Feedback: Sends the guest away with no support. Never dismiss a waiting guest like this.

## Turn 3

**System:** The room becomes ready at 11 AM and you contact the guest in the lounge to let them know.

**Guest:** Oh wonderful, thank you for letting me know so quickly!
**Guest JP:** わあ、早めに教えていただいてありがとうございます！

### Choices

- [2] お待たせいたしました。お部屋の準備が整いましたので、ご案内いたします。お荷物はお部屋までお運びいたします。ご滞在を心よりお楽しみいただけますよう、スタッフ一同おもてなしいたします。
  - EN: Thank you for your patience. Your room is now ready and I will show you the way. We will bring your luggage to the room. Our staff will do everything we can to ensure you have a wonderful stay.
  - Feedback: Perfect. Thanks the guest for waiting, confirms readiness, handles luggage, and closes with a warm and memorable welcome.
- [1] お待たせしました。部屋が準備できました。案内します。
  - EN: Sorry for the wait. Your room is ready. I'll show you.
  - Feedback: Correct and friendly, but the closing lacks the warm send-off that elevates the experience after keeping the guest waiting.
- [0] 準備できました。フロントにカードを取りに来てください。
  - EN: It's ready. Please come to the front desk to pick up the key card.
  - Feedback: Makes the guest come to you rather than going to them. After a wait, escort the guest personally.
- [0] やっと準備できました。
  - EN: It's finally ready.
  - Feedback: やっと (finally) implies the wait was expected or the staff is relieved it's over. Use お待たせいたしました instead.
