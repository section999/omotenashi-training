title: Noise Complaint — Addressing the Noisy Room
description: Staff must visit the room causing noise at 1 AM and politely but firmly ask the guests to quiet down.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** (Opens door) Yeah? What is it?
**Guest JP:** （ドアを開ける）はい？何ですか？

### Choices

- [2] 夜分に突然お邪魔いたしまして大変失礼いたします。他のお客様からお部屋の音が少々気になるとのお声をいただきまして、お伝えにあがりました。お楽しみのところ恐れ入りますが、音量を少々お下げいただくことはできますでしょうか。
  - EN: I am very sorry to disturb you at this late hour. We have received a concern from other guests that some sound is carrying from your room. I apologise for interrupting your evening — would it be possible to lower the volume a little?
  - Feedback: Apologises for the intrusion, explains the reason diplomatically without direct accusation, and makes a polite specific request — this is the correct way to address a noise complaint in person.
- [1] 他のお客様からクレームがありましたので、少し静かにしていただけますか。
  - EN: We have received a complaint from other guests, so could you please keep it down a little?
  - Feedback: Gets the message across, but the word "クレーム" and the direct phrasing can feel blunt and may put the guest on the defensive.
- [0] うるさいので静かにしてください。
  - EN: You are being noisy, so please be quiet.
  - Feedback: Blunt, rude, and lacking any hospitality — this approach will embarrass and anger the guest unnecessarily.
- [0] 苦情が入っています。このままですとご退去いただく場合があります。
  - EN: A complaint has been filed. If this continues, you may be asked to leave.
  - Feedback: Jumping straight to a threat of eviction on the first visit is far too aggressive — escalation of this kind should only be a last resort.

## Turn 2

**System:** The guest is apologetic but slightly dismissive, saying it is not that loud; you must explain other guests have complained and kindly re-request.

**Guest:** Sorry, but honestly it's really not that loud in here. We're just chatting and listening to some music.
**Guest JP:** すみませんが、正直そんなに大きくないと思うんです。ただおしゃべりして音楽を聴いているだけです。

### Choices

- [2] おっしゃる通り、お部屋の中ではそのようにお感じかもしれません。ただ、深夜でございますと廊下や隣のお部屋に音が伝わりやすくなっております。複数のお客様からご連絡をいただいておりますので、重ねてお願いするご無礼をお許しください。どうかもう少しだけ音量をお下げいただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: I completely understand — it may indeed feel that way from inside your room. However, late at night sound tends to travel much more easily through walls and corridors. We have heard from more than one guest, so please forgive me for asking again. Would you be willing to turn the volume down just a little more?
  - Feedback: Acknowledges the guest's perspective genuinely, explains the acoustic reason calmly, mentions multiple complaints without accusation, and makes a gentle but clear second request — diplomatically strong.
- [1] 深夜は音が伝わりやすいため、他の方にご迷惑になっておりますので、ご協力いただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: As sound travels more easily late at night, it is causing a disturbance to others — could we ask for your cooperation?
  - Feedback: Clear and reasonable, but does not fully acknowledge the guest's point of view or apologise for repeating the request.
- [0] 他のお客様が眠れないとおっしゃっています。
  - EN: Other guests say they cannot sleep because of you.
  - Feedback: Attributing the problem directly to this guest in such blunt terms is confrontational and risks an argument.
- [0] ホテルの規則で夜10時以降は静かにしていただくことになっています。
  - EN: Hotel rules require guests to be quiet after 10 PM.
  - Feedback: Citing rules without any human connection sounds authoritarian — this approach is more likely to create resentment than cooperation.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guest agrees but asks if they can keep talking quietly; you confirm that is absolutely fine and wish them a good evening.

**Guest:** OK, OK. We'll keep it down. Can we still talk quietly though?
**Guest JP:** わかりました、わかりました。静かにします。でも、小声でおしゃべりするのはいいですか？

### Choices

- [2] もちろんでございます。お話しいただくことは全く問題ございません。ご協力いただきまして、誠にありがとうございます。夜分にご無礼をお許しください。どうか引き続き楽しい夜をお過ごしくださいませ。
  - EN: Of course — please continue to chat, that is absolutely no problem at all. Thank you very much for your understanding and cooperation. Please forgive the intrusion at this late hour. I hope you enjoy the rest of your evening.
  - Feedback: Warmly confirms what is allowed, expresses genuine gratitude, apologises once more for the interruption, and sends the guest off with a warm wish — a gracious conclusion to a sensitive encounter.
- [1] はい、お話しいただくのは問題ありません。ご協力ありがとうございます。
  - EN: Yes, talking is fine. Thank you for your cooperation.
  - Feedback: Adequately confirms and thanks, but lacks a final apology for the disturbance and a warm send-off.
- [0] はい、でも本当に静かにしてください。また苦情が入るようでしたら対応が変わります。
  - EN: Yes, but please genuinely keep quiet. If more complaints come in, our response will change.
  - Feedback: Adding a veiled threat after the guest has cooperated destroys the goodwill achieved and leaves the interaction on an unnecessarily negative note.
- [0] わかりました。では。
  - EN: Understood. Right then.
  - Feedback: Abruptly ending the conversation with no warmth or gratitude after the guest has agreed is curt and unprofessional.
