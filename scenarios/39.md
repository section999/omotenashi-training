## Turn 1

**Guest:** It's 2 AM and someone is having a loud party nearby.
**Guest JP:** 夜中の2時なのに、近くで大きなパーティーが開かれています。

### Choices

- [2] 深夜にご迷惑をおかけして、大変申し訳ございません。ただちに対応いたします。
  - EN: We are very sorry for the disturbance at this late hour. We will deal with it immediately.
  - Feedback: Urgent, empathetic response to late-night call.
- [1] 分かりました。行きます。
  - EN: Understood. I will go.
  - Feedback: Will act but lacks apology for late-night disturbance.
- [0] どの部屋ですか？
  - EN: Which room is it?
  - Feedback: Should apologize before asking for details.
- [0] うるさいですね。
  - EN: That is noisy.
  - Feedback: Casual empathy without action.

## Turn 2

**System:** You apologize deeply for the late-night disturbance and commit to handling it immediately.

**Guest:** I have an early meeting tomorrow. I really need to sleep.
**Guest JP:** 明日朝早くから会議があります。どうしても眠らないといけないんです。

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。すぐに係の者が静粛にご協力いただくよう伝えます。しばらくお待ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. I will send someone right away to ask for quiet. Please wait just a moment.
  - Feedback: Commits to immediate action with a polite wait.
- [1] 対応します。
  - EN: I will take care of it.
  - Feedback: Will act but no acknowledgment of urgency.
- [0] 他の部屋に移りますか？
  - EN: Would you like to move to another room?
  - Feedback: Moving is a last resort. Solve the noise first.
- [0] 明日対応します。
  - EN: I will deal with it tomorrow.
  - Feedback: Completely unacceptable at 2 AM.

## Turn 3

**System:** You acknowledge the urgency of the early meeting and dispatch staff to quiet the disturbance.

**Guest:** Thank you. I hope it stops soon.
**Guest JP:** ありがとうございます。すぐに止まるといいですね。

### Choices

- [2] おやすみなさいませ。静粛にいたしますのでどうぞご安心ください。また何かございましたら、いつでもフロントまでご連絡くださいませ。
  - EN: Good night. We will ensure it quiets down, so please rest easy. Please contact the front desk anytime if anything else comes up.
  - Feedback: Soothes the guest with a confident commitment, not just a vague hope.
- [1] はい、頑張ります。
  - EN: Yes, I'll do my best.
  - Feedback: Positive intent, slightly casual.
- [0] 多分止まります。
  - EN: It will probably stop.
  - Feedback: Uncertain phrasing. Sound more confident.
- [0] 明日また確認します。
  - EN: I will check again tomorrow.
  - Feedback: Problem should be resolved tonight, not tomorrow.
