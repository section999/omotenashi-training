id: 89
title: Guest with Dementia Wandering the Lobby
description: A front desk staff member notices an elderly guest wandering the lobby looking confused and unable to remember their room number.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** (Wandering, looking uncertain) Excuse me… I'm not sure where I am. I think I'm looking for… I can't remember.
**Guest JP:** （うろうろして困った様子で）すみません……どこにいるか分からなくて。えっと……思い出せなくて。

### Choices

- [2] 大丈夫ですよ。ここはホテルでございます。よろしければ、少しこちらにお座りいただけますか。お名前やお部屋のことなど、一緒に考えてみましょう。どうかご安心ください。
  - EN: You are perfectly safe. This is the hotel. Would you like to have a seat here with me? We can figure things out together. Please do not worry at all.
  - Feedback: Immediately reassures the guest they are safe, uses a calm and gentle tone, invites them to sit, and frames the next step as a shared task rather than an interrogation — precisely the right approach to a confused and vulnerable guest.
- [1] こちらにどうぞ。お部屋番号をご存知ですか？
  - EN: Come this way. Do you know your room number?
  - Feedback: Guides the guest to a seat, which is good, but immediately asking for the room number may cause distress if they cannot remember.
- [0] お客様でしょうか？ご予約はございますか？
  - EN: Are you a guest? Do you have a reservation?
  - Feedback: Treating a clearly disoriented elderly person as a potential non-guest rather than offering immediate care is cold and inappropriate.
- [0] ご家族はどちらにいらっしゃいますか？
  - EN: Where is your family?
  - Feedback: Asking about family as a first response to a confused guest before establishing safety and comfort puts pressure on them to recall information they may not have.

## Turn 2

**System:** The guest cannot recall their name or room number; you search the system by physical description and find a likely match, then call the room and a family member answers.

**Guest:** (On the phone) Hello, yes — that's my grandmother. She has dementia. We've been looking for her! Which floor are you on?
**Guest JP:** （電話で）はい、もしもし——それは私の祖母です！認知症があるんです。ずっと探していました！何階にいますか？

### Choices

- [2] ご安心ください。おばあ様はただいまフロントデスクのそばで、スタッフと一緒に安全にいらっしゃいます。どうかお急ぎにならず、ゆっくりお越しください。1階のフロントデスクでお待ちしております。おばあ様はとても穏やかにしていらっしゃいます。
  - EN: Please be reassured — your grandmother is right here by the front desk with our staff and she is completely safe. Please come when you are ready — there is no need to rush. We are at the main floor front desk. She is calm and comfortable.
  - Feedback: Immediately relieves the family's anxiety, confirms safety, gives a clear location, and reassures about the grandmother's state — provides everything a worried family member needs to hear.
- [1] 1階のフロントにいらっしゃいます。安全ですのでご安心ください。
  - EN: She is at the front desk on the ground floor. She is safe, please do not worry.
  - Feedback: Confirms location and safety, but does not fully convey the warmth and personal care being given.
- [0] 早くいらしてください。お客様のご対応に困っております。
  - EN: Please come quickly. We are struggling to manage the guest.
  - Feedback: Implying the guest is a burden rather than reassuring the family and expressing that the situation is under control is deeply insensitive.
- [0] 次からはご家族から目を離さないようにしてください。
  - EN: In future, please make sure not to let your family member out of sight.
  - Feedback: Criticising the family over the phone while they are frightened and rushing to find their loved one is unkind and unhelpful.

## Turn 3

**System:** The family member rushes down and the grandmother is safely reunited; you reassure the family and offer support for the rest of the stay.

**Guest:** (Family member, visibly shaken) Oh grandmother, there you are. Thank you so much. I'm so sorry for the trouble.
**Guest JP:** （家族が明らかに動揺して）おばあちゃん、よかった。本当にありがとうございます。ご迷惑をおかけして申し訳ありません。

### Choices

- [2] 無事にお会いになれて、何よりでございます。どうかお気になさらないでください。おばあ様は最初からとても穏やかにしていらっしゃいました。ご滞在中に何かご不安なことがあれば、いつでもフロントまでご連絡ください。特別なサポートが必要でしたら、喜んでご相談に応じます。
  - EN: I am so relieved and happy to see you together again. Please do not apologise at all — it was our absolute pleasure to be here. Your grandmother was calm and comfortable from the very beginning. If there is anything we can do to support you during the rest of your stay, please do not hesitate to come to the front desk. We are always here.
  - Feedback: Immediately deflects the apology, reassures about the grandmother's comfort throughout the incident, and proactively offers ongoing support — closing with a genuinely inclusive and caring message.
- [1] よかったですね。ご心配をおかけしました。何かあればフロントまでお声がけください。
  - EN: I am glad you are together. Sorry for any worry. Please let us know at the front desk if you need anything.
  - Feedback: Warm and practical, but the apology is slightly misplaced — the hotel assisted well — and the offer of support could be more specific and personal.
- [0] ご高齢の方のお一人でのご移動には十分お気をつけください。
  - EN: Please be careful about elderly family members moving around alone.
  - Feedback: Offering advice on managing a family member with dementia, however well-intentioned, can feel like a judgment when the family is already distressed — focus on care and support, not guidance.
- [0] こちらも対応が大変でした。
  - EN: It was quite a difficult situation for us to handle as well.
  - Feedback: Drawing attention to the inconvenience caused to staff rather than focusing entirely on the family's wellbeing is entirely inappropriate.
