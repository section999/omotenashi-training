---
id: 79
title: Guest Reports Feeling Ill After Hotel Meal
description: Guest calls to report they believe they got food poisoning from last night's hotel dinner.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** I need to report something urgent. I've been sick all night — nausea and stomach cramps — and I'm certain it was the fish dish I had at your restaurant last night.
**Guest JP:** 至急お伝えしたいことがあります。昨夜からずっと体調が悪くて、吐き気とお腹の痛みがあります。昨夜のレストランで食べた魚料理が原因だと思います。

### Choices

- [2] ご連絡いただきありがとうございます。体調がすぐれない中、大変おつらい思いをされていることと存じます。誠に申し訳ございません。すぐにホテルの医師をお部屋にお伺いさせますので、まずはご安静になさってください。また、飲食部門の責任者にも直ちに報告いたします。
  - EN: Thank you for letting us know. I am so sorry you have been feeling unwell — that must have been a truly terrible night. I am arranging for our hotel doctor to visit your room immediately. Please rest for now. I am also escalating this to our Food and Beverage manager straight away.
  - Feedback: Prioritises the guest's wellbeing above all, apologises sincerely, arranges medical help immediately, and escalates correctly — a complete and caring first response to a serious report.
- [1] 大変申し訳ございません。すぐにお医者様をお呼びします。お部屋番号を教えてください。
  - EN: I am very sorry. I will call the doctor right away. Could I have your room number?
  - Feedback: Sends the doctor promptly and is apologetic, but does not acknowledge the severity with enough empathy or mention the F&B escalation.
- [0] 食中毒であるかどうかは医師が確認するまでは断言できません。
  - EN: We cannot confirm whether it is food poisoning until a doctor has verified it.
  - Feedback: Immediately disputing the guest's description of their own symptoms is defensive and unhelpful — attend to their welfare first.
- [0] 昨日の料理に問題はなかったと思います。
  - EN: I do not believe there was any problem with yesterday's food.
  - Feedback: Defending the kitchen before any investigation has taken place dismisses a guest who is currently ill and risks a serious escalation.

## Turn 2

**System:** You sincerely apologise, offer to send a hotel doctor immediately, and escalate to the F&B manager; the guest is in pain and asks if others have reported the same issue.

**Guest:** It's really bad. Has anyone else complained about the fish from last night?
**Guest JP:** かなりつらいです。昨夜の魚料理について他に誰か苦情を言いましたか？

### Choices

- [2] 大変おつらいことと存じます。誠に申し訳ございません。他のお客様からの同様のご報告については、現在確認中でございます。飲食部門の責任者がすでに昨夜のメニューの調査を開始しております。何よりもまずお客様のご体調が心配でございます。お医者様がただいまお向かいしております。
  - EN: I am so sorry you are in such pain. Regarding other guests, we are currently checking right now — our F&B manager has already begun reviewing last night's menu. Most importantly, your health is our priority. The doctor is on their way to you.
  - Feedback: Acknowledges the guest's pain empathetically, is honest about what is being checked without making premature statements, keeps the focus on the guest's immediate welfare, and confirms the doctor is coming — well balanced.
- [1] 現在確認中です。まずお医者様が参りますので、ご安心ください。
  - EN: We are currently checking. For now, please be reassured the doctor is on their way.
  - Feedback: Concise and reassuring on the key point, but does not sufficiently address the guest's question about other reports.
- [0] 他のお客様からはご連絡をいただいておりません。
  - EN: We have not received any other reports from guests.
  - Feedback: Making a definitive statement before investigations are complete could later prove false and damage trust — honest uncertainty is the right approach.
- [0] そのような情報はお客様にはお伝えできません。
  - EN: That kind of information cannot be shared with guests.
  - Feedback: Refusing to engage with a reasonable question from a sick guest feels secretive and will increase their anxiety.

## Turn 3

**System:** The doctor visits and confirms mild food intolerance rather than food poisoning; you follow up, offer complimentary dining and a formal apology letter from the manager.

**Guest:** The doctor says it might just be a food intolerance. I'm feeling a bit better. Thank you for sending him so quickly.
**Guest JP:** 先生によると食物不耐症かもしれないとのことです。少し楽になりました。すぐに来ていただいてありがとうございます。

### Choices

- [2] お体が回復されていると伺い、安堵いたしました。ご不快をおかけいたしましたこと、改めて心よりお詫び申し上げます。お詫びの気持ちとして、本日以降のお食事を無料でご提供させていただきたく存じます。また、当ホテルの総支配人より正式なお詫びの書面をお届けいたします。どうかお体にお気をつけくださいませ。
  - EN: I am so relieved to hear you are feeling a little better. We once again sincerely apologise for the discomfort you experienced. As an expression of our apologies, we would like to offer you complimentary dining for the remainder of your stay. We will also have a formal letter of apology from our General Manager delivered to your room. Please do take care of yourself.
  - Feedback: Expresses genuine relief, re-apologises for the experience, offers two concrete goodwill gestures, and closes with personal care — a thorough and gracious follow-up.
- [1] お体が回復されて何よりです。今後のお食事を無料でご提供させていただきます。ご不便をおかけしました。
  - EN: I am glad you are feeling better. We will provide complimentary meals going forward. I apologise for the inconvenience.
  - Feedback: Delivers the key goodwill gesture and apology, but does not mention the formal apology letter and lacks the personal warmth of a fuller response.
- [0] 食物不耐症でしたので、料理に問題はなかったことになります。
  - EN: Since it was a food intolerance, it means there was no issue with the food.
  - Feedback: Using the diagnosis to clear the hotel's name rather than focusing on the guest's experience is defensive and will feel dismissive after a difficult night.
- [0] 今後はアレルギーや不耐症についてご申告いただけますとよかったと思います。
  - EN: In future, it would be helpful if you declared any allergies or intolerances.
  - Feedback: Advising the guest to declare intolerances as if this was their fault is inappropriate and unkind following an experience of illness.
