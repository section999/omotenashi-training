---
id: 85
title: Group Tour Coordination Problem
description: A tour group of 20 has arrived but 6 rooms are not yet ready at 2 PM, ahead of the 3 PM check-in time.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hi, I'm the tour guide for a group of 20. We've just arrived and we need our rooms now — some of my guests are elderly and very tired from travelling.
**Guest JP:** こんにちは、20名のグループのツアーガイドです。到着したばかりですが、今すぐ部屋が必要です。お年寄りのお客様がいて、旅の疲れでとても疲れています。

### Choices

- [2] ようこそお越しいただきました。お疲れのことと存じます。ただいまお部屋の準備状況を確認いたします。準備の整ったお部屋からご案内できますよう、すぐに手配いたします。高齢のお客様を優先にご案内いたしますので、少々お待ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Welcome, and thank you for your patience after your journey. I can see your guests are tired. I am checking room status right now. I will arrange for guests to be assigned to ready rooms immediately, prioritising your elderly guests. Please give me just a moment.
  - Feedback: Acknowledges the group's tiredness with empathy, prioritises the most vulnerable guests, and takes immediate action — the correct and guest-focused first response.
- [1] 確認いたします。準備ができたお部屋からご案内いたします。
  - EN: I will check. I will assign rooms as they become ready.
  - Feedback: Practical and correct, but lacks warmth and does not acknowledge the special needs of the elderly guests.
- [0] チェックインは午後3時からとなっております。
  - EN: Check-in is from 3 PM.
  - Feedback: Citing the check-in time to a group of tired elderly travellers without offering any comfort or accommodation shows a lack of compassion.
- [0] 全部屋の準備ができましたら、まとめてご案内いたします。
  - EN: When all rooms are ready, we will check you all in together.
  - Feedback: Making elderly and tired guests wait until every room is ready instead of phasing them in as rooms become available is needlessly inflexible.

## Turn 2

**System:** You arrange the ready rooms for elderly guests first and offer the lounge and refreshments to those still waiting; the tour guide asks about dinner arrangements for the group tonight.

**Guest:** Thank you for that. Can you also confirm the meal arrangements for the group this evening?
**Guest JP:** ありがとうございます。今夜のグループの食事の手配についても確認できますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。ご夕食につきましては、本日の夕食のご予約内容を確認いたします。グループのご予約が入っている場合は詳細をご案内いたします。もしまだご手配がお済みでない場合は、本日でも対応可能かレストランに確認いたします。お時間やご要望などございましたらお聞かせください。
  - EN: Certainly. I will check the dinner reservations for your group. If a group booking is already in place, I will confirm the details for you. If arrangements have not yet been made, I will check with the restaurant whether we can accommodate the group this evening. Please let me know your preferred time and any special requirements.
  - Feedback: Covers both the existing booking check and the contingency if no booking exists, asks for the practical details needed, and shows full ownership of the coordination — strong multi-step response.
- [1] レストランのご予約状況を確認いたします。ご希望の時間はございますか？
  - EN: I will check the restaurant booking status. Do you have a preferred time?
  - Feedback: Takes the right first step and asks a useful question, but does not address what will happen if no booking is found.
- [0] 夕食のご予約はお客様ご自身でレストランに直接お問い合わせください。
  - EN: Please contact the restaurant directly for dinner reservations.
  - Feedback: Deflecting a group coordination question back to the guide when they are already managing 20 tired guests is unhelpful — the hotel should coordinate this centrally.
- [0] 今夜のご予約は確認できていません。
  - EN: I have not been able to confirm tonight's reservation.
  - Feedback: Reporting a lack of information without offering to actively investigate or resolve it is a dead end — always pair a limitation with an action.

## Turn 3

**System:** All rooms are ready by 3:30 PM; you inform the tour guide and arrange a bellhop escort for each guest.

**Guest:** Wonderful. Everyone's been very patient. Can we get all 20 checked in smoothly now?
**Guest JP:** 素晴らしい。皆さんよく我慢してくれました。20名全員をスムーズにチェックインできますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。全室の準備が整いました。各お部屋へのご案内のためにベルスタッフを複数名お呼びしております。また、チェックイン手続きを複数のフロントスタッフで対応し、お待たせする時間を最小限にいたします。お荷物は全員分責任を持ってお届けいたします。お客様の皆様に改めてお詫びとともに、お待ちいただいたことへの感謝をお伝えください。
  - EN: Certainly — all rooms are now ready. I have called several bellhops to escort each guest to their room. We will also open additional front desk positions to minimise waiting time for check-in. All luggage will be delivered personally to each room. Please convey our apologies to your guests and our sincere thanks for their patience.
  - Feedback: Coordinates the full check-in process efficiently, deploys multiple staff, handles luggage, and asks the guide to relay an apology to the group — thorough group service management.
- [1] 準備ができました。ベルスタッフがご案内いたします。チェックイン手続きを開始いたします。
  - EN: Everything is ready. The bellhops will escort guests. We will begin check-in now.
  - Feedback: Confirms readiness and activates the bellhops, but does not address multiple front desk staffing or acknowledge the guests who waited.
- [0] 列に並んでいただいてチェックインを進めてください。
  - EN: Please have guests line up to proceed with check-in.
  - Feedback: Making 20 tired guests — including elderly travellers — queue up with no personalised assistance after a wait is a poor end to a difficult arrival.
- [0] お部屋の鍵はまとめてお渡しします。ガイドさんが配ってください。
  - EN: I will give all the keys to you at once. Please distribute them to your guests.
  - Feedback: Giving the guide a bundle of keys to distribute themselves is operationally lazy and removes the personal welcome each guest deserves on arrival.
