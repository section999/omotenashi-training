## Turn 1

**Guest:** Can I get a wake-up call at 6 AM?
**Guest JP:** 朝6時にモーニングコールをお願いできますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。明朝6時にモーニングコールをご用意いたします。
  - EN: Certainly. I will arrange a wake-up call for 6 AM tomorrow morning.
  - Feedback: Crisp confirmation with polite keigo.
- [1] はい、6時に電話します。
  - EN: Yes, I will call at 6.
  - Feedback: Correct but casual.
- [0] アラームを使ってください。
  - EN: Please use an alarm.
  - Feedback: Dismissive. This is a standard service.
- [0] 6時は早いですね。
  - EN: 6 AM is quite early, isn't it.
  - Feedback: Inappropriate personal comment.

## Turn 2

**System:** You confirm the 6 AM wake-up call has been scheduled for the following morning.

**Guest:** Could you make it 6:30 instead? I might need a little more sleep.
**Guest JP:** 6時30分に変えていただけますか？もう少し眠りたいので。

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。6時30分に変更いたします。ゆっくりとお休みください。
  - EN: Certainly. I will change it to 6:30. Please get a good rest.
  - Feedback: Accommodates the change warmly.
- [1] はい、6時30分にします。
  - EN: Yes, I'll make it 6:30.
  - Feedback: Correct but minimal.
- [0] もう設定しました。変えられません。
  - EN: It has already been set. I cannot change it.
  - Feedback: Inflexible. Always accommodate changes.
- [0] 早く起きた方がいいですよ。
  - EN: It would be better to wake up early.
  - Feedback: Unsolicited advice is inappropriate.

## Turn 3

**System:** You update the wake-up call to 6:30 AM and wish the guest a good rest.
