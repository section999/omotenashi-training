---
id: 81
title: Child Briefly Separated from Parents
description: Parents arrive at the front desk in a panic because their 5-year-old child is missing in the hotel.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Please help us! Our daughter is missing — she's 5 years old. We can't find her anywhere!
**Guest JP:** 助けてください！娘がいなくなりました。5歳です。どこにも見当たりません！

### Choices

- [2] ただいますぐ対応いたします。ご安心ください。全スタッフに無線で連絡し、ホテル全体で捜索を開始いたします。お嬢様のお名前、服装、最後に見かけた場所をお聞かせいただけますでしょうか。同時に防犯カメラの確認も行います。
  - EN: We are acting right now — please try to stay calm. I am alerting all staff by radio immediately and beginning a hotel-wide search. Could you tell me your daughter's name, what she is wearing, and where you last saw her? We are also checking the security cameras at the same time.
  - Feedback: Responds with immediate action and calm authority, reassures the parents, asks for all the key information at once, and describes every step being taken — this is precisely how to respond to a child safety emergency.
- [1] すぐに連絡します。お子様のお名前と服装を教えてください。
  - EN: I will contact staff right away. Please tell me your child's name and what she is wearing.
  - Feedback: Takes the right initial steps but does not convey the full breadth of the response or reassure the parents sufficiently in an extremely stressful moment.
- [0] もう少し探してみてください。ホテルは広いので。
  - EN: Please keep looking a little more. The hotel is large.
  - Feedback: Telling panicking parents to keep looking themselves instead of immediately initiating a coordinated hotel-wide search is an unacceptable response to a child safety emergency.
- [0] お子様はどこかで遊んでいらっしゃるのではないでしょうか。
  - EN: Your child is probably playing somewhere.
  - Feedback: Dismissing parental concern with speculation is deeply inappropriate — treat every report of a missing child with maximum urgency.

## Turn 2

**System:** You immediately alert all hotel staff via radio and check CCTV; a staff member finds the child safe in the gift shop.

**Guest:** (Anxiously waiting) Have you found her? Is she OK?
**Guest JP:** （不安そうに待ちながら）見つかりましたか？大丈夫ですか？

### Choices

- [2] ご安心ください。たったいまスタッフよりご連絡が入りました。お嬢様はギフトショップで安全にお過ごしです。お怪我もなく、スタッフがそばにおります。すぐにご案内いたします。
  - EN: Please be reassured — I have just received word from our staff. Your daughter is safe in the gift shop. She is not hurt, and a member of our team is with her right now. I will take you to her immediately.
  - Feedback: Delivers the good news clearly and at once, confirms she is unhurt and supervised, and immediately takes action to reunite the family — the ideal response to a frantic parent's question.
- [1] 見つかりました。ギフトショップにいます。ご案内します。
  - EN: She has been found. She is in the gift shop. I will take you there.
  - Feedback: Gets to the key information quickly and acts, but could be warmer and more reassuring about the child's wellbeing.
- [0] まだ確認中です。もう少しお待ちください。
  - EN: We are still checking. Please wait a little longer.
  - Feedback: When the child has actually been located, keeping the parents in further suspense without sharing the update immediately is unacceptable.
- [0] ギフトショップにいたみたいです。なぜそこに行ったんでしょうね。
  - EN: It seems she was in the gift shop. I wonder why she went there.
  - Feedback: Speculating on the child's actions rather than immediately escorting the parents to her is a needless delay in a very emotionally charged moment.

## Turn 3

**System:** The child is safely reunited with the parents; you offer reassurance and escort them back to their room.

**Guest:** (Hugging their daughter, visibly relieved) Oh thank goodness. I'm so sorry for the panic.
**Guest JP:** （娘を抱きしめながら、明らかにほっとして）ああ、よかった。大騒ぎして申し訳ありません。

### Choices

- [2] ご無事で本当によかったございました。お子様がお元気でスタッフも安堵いたしました。どうかお気になさらないでください。お部屋までお送りいたします。何かご不安なことがございましたら、いつでもフロントへご連絡くださいませ。
  - EN: We are so relieved she is safe and well — the whole team is. Please do not apologise at all. I will escort you back to your room. If you need anything at all or would like to talk, please do not hesitate to contact the front desk.
  - Feedback: Expresses shared relief warmly, deflects the parent's apology so they do not feel embarrassed, offers an escort, and leaves the door open for further support — a compassionate and gracious close.
- [1] よかったです。お部屋まで一緒に参りましょう。
  - EN: I am glad she is safe. Let me walk with you to your room.
  - Feedback: Warm and kind, but does not explicitly reassure the parents or deflect their apology — a small but meaningful missed opportunity.
- [0] 次はお子様から目を離さないようにしてください。
  - EN: Please make sure not to take your eyes off your child next time.
  - Feedback: Lecturing relieved parents about supervision is judgmental and unkind — the situation has been resolved and the family needs warmth, not a reprimand.
- [0] では、解決しましたので。
  - EN: Well then, that is resolved.
  - Feedback: A cold, transactional sign-off after a deeply emotional incident shows a complete lack of empathy and human connection.
