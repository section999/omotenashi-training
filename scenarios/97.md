## Turn 1

**System:** An emergency call comes in from a guest — her husband has collapsed and is unconscious.

**Guest:** Help! My husband collapsed in the room! He's not responding!
**Guest JP:** 助けてください！主人が部屋で倒れました！意識がないんです！

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。今すぐ救急に連絡いたします。お部屋番号をお教えください。スタッフもすぐに参ります。
  - EN: Understood. I am calling emergency services right now. Please tell me your room number. Our staff will be with you immediately.
  - Feedback: Calm and decisive — three critical actions are communicated at once: 119 is being called, room number is needed, and help is on the way.
- [1] 落ち着いてください。今すぐ医療スタッフを向かわせます。お部屋番号は？
  - EN: Please stay calm. I am sending medical staff immediately. What is your room number?
  - Feedback: Reassuring and action-oriented — confirming that 119 is being called would make this response complete.
- [0] ご主人様の状態を詳しく教えていただけますか？
  - EN: Could you describe your husband's condition in more detail?
  - Feedback: In a life-threatening emergency, gathering information before acting can cost precious seconds — call 119 first.
- [0] フロントスタッフを1名お送りいたします。
  - EN: We will send one front desk staff member.
  - Feedback: A front desk staff member alone is insufficient — 119 and trained first-aid staff must be dispatched without delay.

## Turn 2

**System:** Emergency services (119) have been called. A first-aid-trained staff member is on the way. Staff stays on the line with the guest.

**Guest:** He's breathing but won't wake up. What should I do?
**Guest JP:** 呼吸はしています。でも目が覚めません。どうしたらいいですか？

### Choices

- [2] 呼吸があることは良い状態です。横向きに寝かせて気道を確保してください。体を強く揺さぶらないでください。救急隊員がまもなく到着します。ドアを開けておいていただけますか？
  - EN: It is a good sign that he is breathing. Please lay him on his side to keep his airway clear, and do not shake him. Paramedics will arrive very soon — could you please leave the door open?
  - Feedback: Delivers calm, accurate first-aid guidance and asks the guest to unlock the door — a critical and thoughtful detail.
- [1] 動かさず、そのままお待ちください。スタッフがすぐに参ります。
  - EN: Please do not move him and wait there. Staff will arrive shortly.
  - Feedback: Safe advice, but providing basic airway guidance would be far more helpful in this situation.
- [0] ご主人様の名前を呼び続けてみてください。
  - EN: Keep calling your husband's name.
  - Feedback: Not standard first-aid guidance — follow recognised recovery protocols rather than improvised advice.
- [0] 今すぐ119番に電話してください。
  - EN: Please call 119 immediately.
  - Feedback: The front desk should have called 119 already — telling the guest to do it implies a failure to act.

## Turn 3

**System:** Paramedics have arrived and taken over. The guest is distressed and asks which hospital they're going to.

**Guest:** Which hospital are they taking him to? Can someone come with me?
**Guest JP:** どこの病院に運ばれますか？どなたか一緒に来ていただけますか？

### Choices

- [2] 救急隊員に搬送先の病院を確認いたします。スタッフがタクシーを手配してご案内いたします。パスポートなど必要なものはお手元にございますか？
  - EN: I will confirm the hospital with the paramedics right now. Our staff will arrange a taxi and accompany you. Do you have your passport and any other essentials with you?
  - Feedback: Covers all critical bases — confirms the destination, arranges transport, and reminds the guest of essential items. Exemplary crisis support.
- [1] 病院名は救急隊員にご確認ください。こちらで車をご手配いたします。
  - EN: Please check the hospital name with the paramedics. We will arrange a vehicle for you.
  - Feedback: Functional, but the guest is in shock — doing more of this on their behalf shows genuine care.
- [0] 病院まではタクシーでお越しください。
  - EN: Please take a taxi to the hospital.
  - Feedback: Simply telling a distressed guest to take a taxi is cold — arrange it and offer to accompany them.
- [0] スタッフの同行はご対応しかねます。
  - EN: We are unable to have a staff member accompany you.
  - Feedback: In a genuine medical emergency, showing this level of indifference is unacceptable — always support the guest.
