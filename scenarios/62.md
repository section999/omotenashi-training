id: 62
title: Requesting Extra Amenities
description: Guest calls to request extra pillows and a yukata (浴衣).
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hi, can I get two extra pillows and a yukata for my room?
**Guest JP:** すみません、枕を二つと浴衣を部屋に持ってきてもらえますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。枕を二つと浴衣でございますね。お部屋番号をお伺いできますでしょうか。すぐにお届けいたします。
  - EN: Certainly. Two extra pillows and a yukata — is that right? May I have your room number? We will bring them to you shortly.
  - Feedback: Confirms the items clearly with でございますね, requests necessary information, and gives a reassuring delivery promise.
- [1] はい、枕二つと浴衣ですね。少々お待ちください。
  - EN: Yes, two pillows and a yukata. Please wait a moment.
  - Feedback: Accurate and polite, but does not ask for the room number and lacks a warm delivery commitment.
- [0] 浴衣のサイズはおわかりですか？
  - EN: Do you know your yukata size?
  - Feedback: Jumping straight to a size question without first acknowledging the request sounds abrupt.
- [0] フロントにお越しいただければご用意できます。
  - EN: We can have them ready if you come to the front desk.
  - Feedback: Making the guest come downstairs for basic amenities is poor hospitality — in-room delivery is expected.

## Turn 2

**System:** You confirm the two pillows and yukata will be delivered and ask if there is anything else.

**Guest:** Actually, could I also get some extra towels and a toothbrush kit?
**Guest JP:** あと、タオルも何枚かと、歯ブラシのセットも一緒にお願いできますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。タオルと歯ブラシセットも承りました。枕二つ、浴衣、タオル、歯ブラシセットを合わせてお届けいたします。しばらくお待ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. I have noted the towels and toothbrush kit as well. We will deliver the two pillows, yukata, towels, and toothbrush kit together. Please bear with us for a moment.
  - Feedback: Outstanding — lists all four items to confirm, combines delivery efficiently, and closes with a polite request for patience.
- [1] 承りました。タオルと歯ブラシも一緒にお持ちします。
  - EN: Understood. We will bring the towels and toothbrush along as well.
  - Feedback: Clear and correct, but does not repeat all items for full confirmation and lacks a formal closing phrase.
- [0] タオルは何枚必要ですか？
  - EN: How many towels do you need?
  - Feedback: A clarifying question is fine, but asking it before acknowledging the request makes it feel transactional rather than hospitable.
- [0] 歯ブラシは一つでいいですか？
  - EN: Just one toothbrush — is that right?
  - Feedback: Questioning the quantity without a warm acknowledgment sounds interrogative and unwelcoming.

## Turn 3

**System:** All requested items have been delivered to the room and a staff member follows up.

**Guest:** Everything arrived — thank you so much! It's all perfect.
**Guest JP:** 全部届きました。ありがとうございます！完璧です。

### Choices

- [2] ありがとうございます。お役に立てて光栄でございます。他にご入用のものがございましたら、どうぞお気軽にお申し付けくださいませ。ゆっくりおくつろぎください。
  - EN: Thank you very much. It is an honour to be of service. Please feel free to let us know if there is anything else you need. Please enjoy your stay.
  - Feedback: Warm, gracious, and complete — thanks the guest, expresses pride in service, and invites further requests with a heartfelt closing.
- [1] よかったです。何かあればまたご連絡ください。
  - EN: Glad to hear it. Please contact us again if you need anything.
  - Feedback: Friendly and functional, but lacks the polish of a keigo-level closing that reflects five-star hospitality.
- [0] はい、わかりました。
  - EN: Yes, understood.
  - Feedback: A minimal reply that gives no warmth to the interaction — fails to leave the guest with a positive impression.
- [0] 不足がなければ結構です。
  - EN: If nothing is missing, that is fine.
  - Feedback: Sounds indifferent and perfunctory — entirely unsuitable as a closing response to a happy guest.
