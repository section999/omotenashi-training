---
id: 72
title: Late Cancellation Fee Dispute
description: Guest calls to dispute a 100% cancellation fee on a non-refundable booking, citing a family emergency.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** I need to dispute a cancellation charge on my booking. I had a family emergency and had to cancel.
**Guest JP:** キャンセル料について異議を申し立てたいのですが。家族の緊急事態があってキャンセルしなければなりませんでした。

### Choices

- [2] この度はご連絡いただきありがとうございます。まず、ご家族のご事情についてお見舞い申し上げます。ご不便をおかけし誠に申し訳ございません。詳しくお話を伺いながら、ご予約内容を確認させていただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: Thank you for contacting us. First, I would like to express my sincere concern for your family situation. I am sorry for the inconvenience this has caused. May I listen to the details while I look into your reservation?
  - Feedback: Leads with empathy for the family situation before turning to the booking — this human-first approach is essential when a guest has experienced a personal emergency.
- [1] ご予約番号をお聞かせいただけますか。確認いたします。
  - EN: May I have your booking number? I will check the details.
  - Feedback: Moves directly to the practical check without acknowledging the guest's difficult circumstances — technically correct but lacks warmth.
- [0] キャンセル料は規約に定められていますので、ご返金はできません。
  - EN: The cancellation fee is set out in our terms, so a refund is not possible.
  - Feedback: Immediately quoting policy before hearing the guest out is dismissive and will instantly escalate the call.
- [0] キャンセルのお手続きはいつされましたか？
  - EN: When did you carry out the cancellation?
  - Feedback: Jumping to a factual interrogation without any acknowledgment of the emergency feels cold and transactional.

## Turn 2

**System:** You check the booking and confirm it is 100% non-refundable per hotel policy; the guest becomes emotional and reveals it was a death in the family.

**Guest:** My mother passed away. I had to fly home immediately. Surely you can make an exception?
**Guest JP:** 母が亡くなったんです。すぐに帰国しなければなりませんでした。例外として対応していただけませんか？

### Choices

- [2] このたびは誠にご愁傷様でございます。心よりお悔やみ申し上げます。ご予約はキャンセル不可のプランでございますが、このようなご事情を考慮し、上席に確認のうえ、できる限りのご対応をいたします。少々お時間をいただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: I am so deeply sorry for your loss. Please accept my sincerest condolences. While your booking is on a non-refundable plan, given your circumstances I would like to consult with my supervisor to see what we can do for you. May I ask for just a little time?
  - Feedback: Offers genuine condolences before addressing the policy, and crucially does not refuse outright — promising to escalate shows that the hotel values the person over the policy.
- [1] ご愁傷様でございます。規約上は返金が難しい状況ですが、上の者に確認してみます。
  - EN: I am sorry for your loss. A refund is difficult under our terms, but I will check with my supervisor.
  - Feedback: Condolences are offered and escalation is promised, but the response feels brief and lacks the warmth and sincerity the situation demands.
- [0] お気持ちはわかりますが、規約ですのでご対応は難しい状況です。
  - EN: I understand your feelings, but this is our policy and it is difficult for us to make an exception.
  - Feedback: Using policy as a wall against a grieving guest is deeply inappropriate — this response will cause a serious complaint.
- [0] 証明書などはご用意いただけますか？
  - EN: Would you be able to provide any documentation?
  - Feedback: Immediately asking a bereaved guest for proof is callous — at minimum, empathy must come first before any process discussion.

## Turn 3

**System:** As a gesture of goodwill, you offer a future stay credit equal to the full booking value instead of a cash refund, and the guest considers and accepts.

**Guest:** A credit… I suppose that's fair. I do plan to visit Japan again. Thank you for understanding.
**Guest JP:** クレジットですか……それは公平だと思います。また日本に来る予定もあります。ご理解いただいてありがとうございます。

### Choices

- [2] ありがとうございます。このたびのご事情に、心よりお見舞い申し上げます。ご宿泊クレジットは1年間有効とさせていただきます。ご利用の際にはご連絡いただけますでしょうか。次回のご来館を心よりお待ち申し上げております。
  - EN: Thank you so much. We offer our deepest sympathy for what you have been through. The stay credit will be valid for one year. When you are ready to use it, please do not hesitate to contact us. We look forward to welcoming you back.
  - Feedback: Closes with warmth and sympathy, states the credit validity clearly, and extends a genuine welcome for the next visit — a compassionate resolution.
- [1] かしこまりました。クレジットのご案内をメールにてお送りします。またのご利用をお待ちしております。
  - EN: Certainly. I will send you the credit details by email. We look forward to your next visit.
  - Feedback: Handles the logistics but misses an opportunity to acknowledge the guest's loss one final time before closing.
- [0] では、クレジットのご利用は通常のご予約ルールに従ってください。
  - EN: Please note that the credit must be used in accordance with our normal booking rules.
  - Feedback: Leading with restrictions at the close of a compassionate conversation is tone-deaf and undoes the goodwill created.
- [0] それで解決ということでよろしいでしょうか。
  - EN: So that resolves the matter, correct?
  - Feedback: Seeking a transactional sign-off without any warmth after a bereavement conversation is deeply inappropriate.
