id: 69
title: Guest Feels Unwell
description: Guest calls from their room saying they feel ill and need assistance.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hi, I'm not feeling well — I have a headache and I feel nauseous. Does the hotel have any medicine I could have?
**Guest JP:** すみません、体調が悪くて…頭痛と吐き気があります。ホテルに薬はありますか？

### Choices

- [2] ご体調がすぐれないとのこと、大変心配でございます。基本的なお薬をお部屋までお持ちいたします。また、もしご必要でしたら、近くの医師にご連絡することもできますが、いかがでしょうか。お部屋番号をお伺いできますでしょうか。
  - EN: I am very concerned to hear you are not feeling well. We will bring some basic medicine to your room. If you need one, we are also able to contact a nearby doctor — would that be helpful? May I ask for your room number?
  - Feedback: Shows genuine concern, offers medicine and the option of a doctor, and asks for the room number — a thorough and compassionate response.
- [1] 承知いたしました。お薬をお持ちします。お部屋番号を教えていただけますか？
  - EN: Understood. I will bring medicine to you. Could you tell me your room number?
  - Feedback: Responds promptly and correctly, but misses the chance to offer a doctor and does not express warmth or concern.
- [0] 薬局はホテルの近くにあります。
  - EN: There is a pharmacy near the hotel.
  - Feedback: Directing an unwell guest to go out and find medicine themselves is unhelpful and indifferent.
- [0] どんな症状ですか？
  - EN: What kind of symptoms do you have?
  - Feedback: The guest already described their symptoms — repeating the question suggests the staff did not listen, which adds frustration to an already uncomfortable situation.

## Turn 2

**System:** You offer to bring basic medicine and ask if the guest would like a doctor to visit.

**Guest:** Medicine should be enough for now, thanks. Could you check on me in an hour, though?
**Guest JP:** 今は薬で大丈夫だと思います。ただ、1時間後に様子を見てもらえますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。ただいまお薬をお届けいたします。1時間後に必ずご連絡差し上げます。ゆっくりお休みいただき、少しでもお楽になれますよう願っております。何かご入用でしたら、いつでもフロントへお申し付けくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. I will bring the medicine to you right now. I will be sure to check on you in one hour. Please rest and I hope you feel better soon. If you need anything at all, please do not hesitate to call the front desk.
  - Feedback: Commits firmly to the follow-up, sends the guest off with a genuine well-wish, and reminds them the front desk is always available — compassionate and complete.
- [1] 承りました。1時間後にご連絡します。お薬はすぐに持っていきます。
  - EN: Understood. I will contact you in an hour. I will bring the medicine right away.
  - Feedback: Covers the key points but lacks a personal expression of care or a warm closing.
- [0] 1時間後ですね、わかりました。
  - EN: One hour later, understood.
  - Feedback: Minimal acknowledgment with no warmth — for a guest who is unwell, this feels cold and indifferent.
- [0] 症状が悪化したら救急車を呼んでください。
  - EN: If your symptoms worsen, please call an ambulance.
  - Feedback: While safety information has its place, leading with this response when the guest has asked for something much simpler is alarmist and unhelpful.

## Turn 3

**System:** One hour later, you call the guest's room to check on their condition.

**Guest:** I'm feeling much better now, thank you so much for checking!
**Guest JP:** だいぶ良くなりました。気にかけてくれてありがとうございます！

### Choices

- [2] それはよかったです。ご回復されたとのこと、心よりほっといたしました。引き続きゆっくりとお休みいただき、もし何かご不安なことがございましたら、いつでもご遠慮なくフロントまでご連絡くださいませ。
  - EN: That is wonderful to hear. I am truly relieved that you are feeling better. Please continue to rest, and if you feel at all uncertain about your condition, please do not hesitate to call the front desk at any time.
  - Feedback: Warm, genuine relief expressed naturally, followed by ongoing care and an open invitation to call again — exemplary follow-up.
- [1] 良くなられてよかったです。何かあればまたご連絡ください。
  - EN: I am glad you are feeling better. Please call us again if you need anything.
  - Feedback: Pleasant and clear, but the expression of relief feels a little formulaic and could be more personal.
- [0] わかりました、ありがとうございました。
  - EN: Understood, thank you.
  - Feedback: Thanking the guest at the close of a welfare check is odd and entirely misses the human moment — the focus should be on the guest's recovery.
- [0] ではまた何かあればどうぞ。
  - EN: Please let us know again if something comes up.
  - Feedback: Casual and dismissive — fails to acknowledge the guest's gratitude or express any genuine care for their wellbeing.
