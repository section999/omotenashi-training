## Turn 1

**Guest:** (answering the phone)
**Guest JP:** はい。

**System:** You call the guest to inform them a package has arrived for them at the front desk.

### Choices

- [2] 〇〇様、フロントでございます。先ほどお客様宛てのお荷物がお届けに参りましたことをご連絡いたしました。ご都合のよろしいときに、フロントまでお越しいただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: This is the front desk calling for Mr./Ms. [Guest Name]. I am calling to let you know that a package has arrived for you. When it is convenient for you, would you be able to come to the front desk to collect it?
  - Feedback: Identifies the caller, states the purpose clearly, and invites the guest at their convenience — polished and considerate.
- [1] フロントです。お荷物が届いています。いつ取りに来られますか？
  - EN: This is the front desk. A package has arrived for you. When can you come to collect it?
  - Feedback: Clear and direct, but "いつ取りに来られますか" sounds slightly demanding — the phrasing could be softer.
- [0] 荷物が来てるので取りに来てください。
  - EN: Your package is here, so please come and get it.
  - Feedback: The blunt imperative phrasing is far too casual and lacks the respect owed to a hotel guest.
- [0] フロントに荷物があります。
  - EN: There is a package at the front desk.
  - Feedback: Factual but cold — no greeting, no courtesy, and no invitation to collect it at their convenience.

## Turn 2

**System:** The guest comes down to the front desk to collect the package.

**Guest:** Hi, could I leave it here at the front desk and pick it up tomorrow morning?
**Guest JP:** あの、明日の朝まで、フロントで預かってもらえますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。明日の朝までお預かりいたします。お名前とお部屋番号を承りますので、お荷物に管理タグをつけさせていただきます。明朝、ご都合のよろしい時間にお越しくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. We will keep the package for you until tomorrow morning. I will take your name and room number so we can attach a storage tag to it. Please come by at whatever time suits you in the morning.
  - Feedback: Accepts the request immediately, explains the handling process, and gives the guest flexibility — thorough and reassuring.
- [1] はい、お預かりいたします。お名前と部屋番号を教えていただけますか？
  - EN: Yes, we will keep it for you. May I have your name and room number?
  - Feedback: Correct procedure, but lacks detail about how the package will be labelled or when to collect it.
- [0] 保管は24時間までです。
  - EN: We can only store it for 24 hours.
  - Feedback: Leading with a restriction rather than a confirmation of service sounds unhelpful and unwelcoming.
- [0] 大きい荷物は難しいかもしれません。
  - EN: Large items may be difficult.
  - Feedback: Raising doubts without first trying to accommodate the request is premature and discouraging.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guest decides to take the package now, but it is clearly heavy.

**Guest:** Actually, I'll take it now. Oh, it's quite heavy...
**Guest JP:** やっぱり今持って行きます。あ、けっこう重いな…

### Choices

- [2] よろしければ、ベルスタッフがお部屋までお運びいたします。ご不便をおかけして申し訳ございませんが、どうぞおまかせくださいませ。
  - EN: If you would like, our bellhop will carry it to your room for you. Please do not hesitate to leave it with us.
  - Feedback: Proactive, considerate, and uses natural keigo — anticipates the guest's need without waiting to be asked.
- [1] お持ちしましょうか？スタッフに運ばせます。
  - EN: Shall we carry it for you? I will have a staff member take it.
  - Feedback: Offers help promptly, though the phrasing is slightly less graceful than ideal.
- [0] エレベーターはあちらです。
  - EN: The elevator is over there.
  - Feedback: Pointing to the elevator rather than offering assistance when the guest is visibly struggling is indifferent and unhelpful.
- [0] 気をつけてお持ちください。
  - EN: Please be careful carrying it.
  - Feedback: Offering only a caution rather than actual help when staff could assist is a missed opportunity for genuine hospitality.
