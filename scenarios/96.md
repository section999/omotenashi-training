id: 96
title: Wi-Fi Not Working
description: Guest reports that the in-room Wi-Fi is down and they have an urgent video call.
---

## Turn 1

**System:** A guest calls the front desk, clearly frustrated that the Wi-Fi in their room is not working.

**Guest:** The Wi-Fi in my room isn't working at all. I have a video call in 30 minutes.
**Guest JP:** 部屋のWi-Fiが全然繋がらないんですが。30分後にビデオ通話があるんです。

### Choices

- [2] ご不便をおかけして大変申し訳ございません。ただいま確認いたします。お部屋番号をお教えいただけますか？
  - EN: We sincerely apologise for the inconvenience. I will look into this right away. May I have your room number?
  - Feedback: Apologises immediately, commits to action, and gathers the essential information — the ideal opening under pressure.
- [1] はい、Wi-Fiの不具合ですね。お部屋番号をお聞かせください。担当者に確認いたします。
  - EN: Yes, a Wi-Fi issue. May I have your room number? I will check with the relevant staff.
  - Feedback: Appropriate, though leading with an apology shows more empathy — especially given the urgent deadline.
- [0] 一度パスワードを再入力してみてください。
  - EN: Please try re-entering the Wi-Fi password.
  - Feedback: Possible troubleshooting, but gather the room number and understand the issue before offering advice.
- [0] 他のお部屋ではご利用いただけております。
  - EN: Wi-Fi is working in other rooms.
  - Feedback: This information does not help the guest — focus on solving their problem, not explaining the scope.

## Turn 2

**System:** A router issue has been identified on that floor. The technical team has been dispatched.

**Guest:** How long will it take? My call is in 25 minutes now.
**Guest JP:** どのくらいかかりますか？もう25分しかないんですが。

### Choices

- [2] 大変申し訳ございません。技術担当がただいま向かっておりまして、15分以内に復旧できる見込みでございます。万が一間に合わない場合は、1階のビジネスセンターの有線LANをご利用いただけますので、ご安心くださいませ。
  - EN: We sincerely apologise. Our technical team is on the way and we expect to restore the connection within 15 minutes. If it is not ready in time, please rest assured you are welcome to use the wired connection in our business centre on the ground floor.
  - Feedback: Gives a clear estimate and immediately offers an alternative solution — exceptional service under pressure.
- [1] 15分ほどで復旧できる予定です。ご不便をおかけして申し訳ございません。
  - EN: We expect to restore it in about 15 minutes. We apologise for the inconvenience.
  - Feedback: Clear and apologetic — offering a backup option in case of delay would make this response complete.
- [0] はっきりとした復旧時間はお伝えできません。
  - EN: I cannot give you an exact restoration time.
  - Feedback: Unacceptable — provide a best estimate or escalate immediately to someone who can commit to one.
- [0] Wi-Fiは無料のサービスですので、対応が遅くなる場合がございます。
  - EN: Wi-Fi is a complimentary service, so response times may be slower.
  - Feedback: Citing the free nature of the service to excuse slow response is completely inappropriate.

## Turn 3

**System:** Wi-Fi is restored within 12 minutes. Staff calls the guest to confirm, and the guest asks about compensation.

**Guest:** It's working now, thank you. But this was really stressful — is there anything you can do?
**Guest JP:** 繋がりました、ありがとうございます。でも本当に焦りましたよ。何かしていただけますか？

### Choices

- [2] ご迷惑をおかけして誠に申し訳ございませんでした。気持ちばかりではございますが、本日のルームサービスを1品サービスさせていただければと存じますが、いかがでしょうか？
  - EN: We are truly sorry for the trouble this caused you. As a small token of our apology, we would like to offer you one complimentary room service item — would that be acceptable?
  - Feedback: Acknowledges the distress sincerely and offers a tangible, proportionate gesture — this is excellent service recovery.
- [1] ご不便をおかけして大変申し訳ございませんでした。担当マネージャーにご要望をお伝えいたします。
  - EN: We are very sorry for the inconvenience. I will pass your concern on to the duty manager.
  - Feedback: Escalating to a manager is appropriate, but making a direct offer now would feel more responsive and personal.
- [0] ご不便はおかけしましたが、補償の対応は難しい状況でございます。
  - EN: We apologise for the inconvenience, but compensation is not possible in this case.
  - Feedback: Refusing without explanation or an alternative is dismissive — consult a manager before declining.
- [0] 復旧できましたので、問題は解決いたしました。
  - EN: The connection has been restored, so the issue is resolved.
  - Feedback: Technically true but tone-deaf — the guest experienced real stress and deserves a meaningful acknowledgement.
