## Turn 1

**Guest:** Excuse me, are there any interesting places to visit around here?
**Guest JP:** すみません、この近くで行く価値のある場所はありますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。近隣には素晴らしいスポットがいくつかございます。特に、歴史ある〇〇寺と、地元の活気ある〇〇市場はぜひお訪ねいただきたいと存じます。ご興味はいかがでしょうか。
  - EN: Certainly. There are several wonderful spots nearby. In particular, I would highly recommend the historic Maruichi Temple and the lively local Maruni Market. Might either of those interest you?
  - Feedback: Confident, specific, and engaging — uses 存じます gracefully and invites the guest into a conversation rather than just listing facts.
- [1] はい、近くに有名なお寺と市場がございます。観光にお勧めです。
  - EN: Yes, there is a famous temple and a market nearby. They are recommended for sightseeing.
  - Feedback: Informative but flat — no personal warmth, no follow-up question to engage the guest further.
- [0] 観光案内所がありますので、そちらへどうぞ。
  - EN: There is a tourist information office, so please head there.
  - Feedback: Passing the guest off to another facility without offering any help yourself is unhelpful for a concierge role.
- [0] どんなものがお好きですか？
  - EN: What kinds of things do you like?
  - Feedback: While tailoring recommendations is good, asking this as the very first response — without offering any suggestion — leaves the guest with nothing.

## Turn 2

**System:** You suggest a famous temple and the local market as top recommendations.

**Guest:** Those sound great! Is there a way to get there without a car?
**Guest JP:** いいですね！車なしで行く方法はありますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。どちらもバスでお越しいただけます。ホテル前のバス停から〇番系統にご乗車いただければ、約15分でお寺に到着いたします。市場はその一つ手前の停留所でございます。地図をご用意いたしますので、お持ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. Both locations can be reached by bus. From the bus stop in front of the hotel, take route number X and you will reach the temple in about 15 minutes. The market is one stop before that. I will prepare a map for you — please take it with you.
  - Feedback: Detailed, practical, and proactive — covers both destinations, gives travel time, and offers a map without being asked.
- [1] バスで行けます。ホテルの前のバス停から乗れますよ。
  - EN: You can take the bus. There is a bus stop right in front of the hotel.
  - Feedback: Correct and helpful, but lacks specific route numbers, travel time, or the offer of a map.
- [0] タクシーの方が便利かもしれません。
  - EN: A taxi might be more convenient.
  - Feedback: Redirecting to a more expensive option without answering the guest's actual question about bus access is unhelpful.
- [0] ちょっと遠いので、難しいかもしれません。
  - EN: It is a bit far, so it might be difficult.
  - Feedback: Vague and discouraging — does not give the guest the information they need and may unnecessarily put them off the visit.

## Turn 3

**System:** You explain the bus route and offer the guest a map of the area.

**Guest:** Thanks! Do you know what time the temple closes?
**Guest JP:** ありがとうございます！お寺は何時に閉まるか知っていますか？

### Choices

- [2] 〇〇寺の閉門は通常17時でございます。季節によって異なる場合もございますので、念のため最新情報をご確認いただくことをお勧めいたします。公式の連絡先をこちらの地図に記載しておきますので、ご活用くださいませ。
  - EN: The temple usually closes at 5 PM. Closing times can vary by season, so I recommend confirming the latest information just to be safe. I will note their contact details on the map for you — please make use of it.
  - Feedback: Accurate and thorough — gives the standard time, flags a potential variation, and adds the contact information proactively.
- [1] たいてい17時に閉まると思います。念のか確認されることをお勧めします。
  - EN: I believe it usually closes at 5 PM. I recommend confirming just in case.
  - Feedback: Honest and reasonable, but less polished — no offer to include the contact on the map or assist further.
- [0] 詳しくはわかりません。
  - EN: I do not know the details.
  - Feedback: A concierge should know or be able to find this information quickly — saying "I don't know" without any offer to check is insufficient.
- [0] ホームページで調べてみてください。
  - EN: Please look it up on their website.
  - Feedback: Telling the guest to do their own research is the opposite of concierge service.
