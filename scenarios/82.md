---
id: 82
title: Media Inquiry About Celebrity Guest
description: A journalist calls asking to confirm whether a well-known celebrity is staying at the hotel.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hi, I'm a journalist with the Daily Post. I'm calling to confirm whether a certain celebrity is currently staying at your hotel.
**Guest JP:** もしもし、デイリー・ポスト紙の記者です。ある有名人が現在御社のホテルに宿泊しているかどうかを確認するためにお電話しています。

### Choices

- [2] ご連絡いただきありがとうございます。誠に申し訳ございませんが、当ホテルでは、いかなるお客様のご宿泊情報につきましても、プライバシーポリシーに基づき確認または否定をいたしかねます。何卒ご理解いただけますようお願い申し上げます。
  - EN: Thank you for calling. I am afraid that in accordance with our privacy policy, we are unable to confirm or deny information about any guest's stay at our hotel. I appreciate your understanding.
  - Feedback: Politely and firmly states the confidentiality policy without being confrontational, declines to confirm or deny, and thanks the caller — the correct and complete response to a media inquiry.
- [1] お客様のご宿泊情報はお伝えできません。
  - EN: We cannot share information about guests' stays.
  - Feedback: Correct in substance but slightly blunt — a more formal phrasing with a rationale would be more professionally appropriate.
- [0] 今はいらっしゃいません。
  - EN: They are not here at the moment.
  - Feedback: Responding with any specific information — even a denial — violates guest confidentiality and gives the journalist something to work with.
- [0] そのような情報は担当部署にお聞きください。
  - EN: Please ask the relevant department for that kind of information.
  - Feedback: Transferring a media privacy inquiry rather than handling it at the first point of contact risks the call reaching someone less trained to handle it.

## Turn 2

**System:** You maintain the hotel's privacy policy; the journalist presses harder, claiming they already have a source.

**Guest:** Look, I already have a source who confirmed the stay. I just need an official comment from the hotel.
**Guest JP:** 実は、すでに宿泊を確認した情報源があります。ホテルからの公式コメントが欲しいだけです。

### Choices

- [2] 情報の出所がいかなるものであれ、当ホテルとしてはお客様のご滞在に関するいかなる情報も公式にお伝えする立場にございません。当ホテルのプライバシーポリシーはすべてのお客様に平等に適用されます。ご取材につきましては、当ホテルの広報担当を通じてのみ対応させていただいております。
  - EN: Regardless of your source, the hotel is not in a position to officially confirm any information about any guest's stay. Our privacy policy applies equally to all guests without exception. Regarding media enquiries, these can only be handled through our public relations department.
  - Feedback: Holds firm without being hostile, acknowledges the claim about a source without engaging with it, and correctly redirects formal media enquiries to PR — professional and composed under pressure.
- [1] それでも当ホテルはご宿泊情報についてお伝えできません。メディアのお問い合わせは広報にお願いします。
  - EN: Even so, we cannot share information about guest stays. Please contact our PR department for media enquiries.
  - Feedback: Maintains the position and redirects correctly, but does not acknowledge why the source claim does not change the hotel's position.
- [0] それはうちのスタッフが情報を漏らしたということですか？
  - EN: Are you saying one of our staff leaked that information?
  - Feedback: Reacting defensively to the mention of a source derails the conversation and implies the hotel is more concerned about an internal leak than about the guest's privacy.
- [0] では、ノーコメントということでよろしいですか。
  - EN: So, no comment — is that right?
  - Feedback: "No comment" is a media shorthand that implies something to hide — always frame the position as a privacy policy, not a refusal to comment.

## Turn 3

**System:** You firmly but politely maintain confidentiality; the journalist now asks to speak to the manager.

**Guest:** Fine. Can I speak to the manager then?
**Guest JP:** わかりました。では、マネージャーと話せますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。ただいま広報担当マネージャーにつなぎます。当ホテルのメディア対応は広報が一元管理しておりますので、詳細はそちらにてご対応いたします。少々お待ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. I will connect you to our Public Relations Manager. All media-related matters at our hotel are handled exclusively through our PR department, and they will be happy to assist you. Please hold for just a moment.
  - Feedback: Grants the request professionally, ensures the call goes to the correct person for media handling, and explains why — a smooth and appropriate handoff.
- [1] マネージャーに代わります。少々お待ちください。
  - EN: I will put you through to the manager. Please hold.
  - Feedback: Connects the journalist to a manager but does not specify it should be the PR manager — risk of connecting to a manager not trained in media handling.
- [0] マネージャーも同じことしか申し上げられません。
  - EN: The manager will only tell you the same thing.
  - Feedback: Discouraging the caller from speaking to a manager sounds dismissive and may appear as if the hotel has something to hide.
- [0] マネージャーは現在対応できません。
  - EN: The manager is unavailable at the moment.
  - Feedback: Claiming unavailability without offering any alternative or follow-up path is evasive and will frustrate a journalist on a deadline.
