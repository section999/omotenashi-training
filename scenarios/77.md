---
id: 77
title: Credit Card Declined at Checkout
description: Guest's credit card is declined at checkout for a 250,000 yen bill.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** (At checkout) Here's my card.
**Guest JP:** （チェックアウト時）こちらがカードです。

### Choices

- [2] ありがとうございます。少々お待ちくださいませ。……大変申し上げにくいのですが、ただいまカードのお手続きができない状況でございます。ご不便をおかけして誠に申し訳ございません。別のカードをお持ちでいらっしゃいますでしょうか、あるいはご確認いただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: Thank you. Please bear with me for just a moment. I am very sorry to have to inform you, but we are unable to process this card at this time. I apologise for the inconvenience. Do you happen to have another card, or would you like a moment to look into it?
  - Feedback: Delivers the difficult news diplomatically without implying the guest's fault, apologises genuinely, and immediately offers a constructive path forward — the right approach to a sensitive payment issue.
- [1] 申し訳ございませんが、カードが通りませんでした。別のカードはございますか？
  - EN: I am sorry, but the card was not approved. Do you have another card?
  - Feedback: Gets to the point and offers an alternative, but could be more apologetic and less blunt about the decline.
- [0] カードが使えません。
  - EN: Your card cannot be used.
  - Feedback: Blunt and unexplained — the guest has no idea why and feels singled out. Always phrase a decline with tact.
- [0] 250,000円のお支払いが必要です。現金はございますか？
  - EN: You need to pay 250,000 yen. Do you have cash?
  - Feedback: Jumping to cash without any diplomatic handling of the declined card is abrupt and potentially embarrassing for the guest.

## Turn 2

**System:** The guest tries a second card and it is also declined; the guest suspects their bank may have flagged a large overseas transaction and asks to call the bank.

**Guest:** That one's declined too? I think my bank flagged it as a large overseas charge. Can I use your phone to call them?
**Guest JP:** こちらも通りませんか？銀行が大きな海外決済としてフラグを立てたと思います。銀行に電話してもいいですか？

### Choices

- [2] もちろんでございます。どうぞこちらの電話をお使いください。またプライバシーのためにこちらの個室でお電話いただくことも可能でございます。お手続きに少々お時間がかかってしまいますこと、誠に申し訳ございません。どうかお急ぎでなければ、ごゆっくりご対応ください。
  - EN: Of course — please use our phone. We also have a private space where you can make the call if you prefer. We sincerely apologise that this is taking a little time. Please take as long as you need.
  - Feedback: Immediately grants the request, offers privacy, apologises for the delay, and relieves pressure — empathetic and practical in equal measure.
- [1] はい、こちらの電話をお使いください。ご不便をおかけします。
  - EN: Yes, please use this phone. I apologise for the inconvenience.
  - Feedback: Grants the request and apologises, but misses the offer of a private space and further reassurance.
- [0] お電話は公衆電話をご利用ください。ロビーにございます。
  - EN: Please use the payphone in the lobby.
  - Feedback: Sending the guest to a payphone to resolve a billing issue caused by the hotel's checkout process is dismissive and unhelpful.
- [0] お支払いが完了するまでお荷物をお預かりすることになります。
  - EN: We will need to hold your luggage until payment is completed.
  - Feedback: Treating the guest as a potential evader by threatening to hold their luggage before the issue is even resolved is deeply inappropriate and accusatory.

## Turn 3

**System:** The bank confirms and authorises the payment — it goes through; you complete checkout warmly and wish the guest well.

**Guest:** It went through! They just needed to confirm it was me. Sorry for the hassle.
**Guest JP:** 通りました！本人確認が必要だっただけです。ご面倒をおかけして申し訳ありません。

### Choices

- [2] ありがとうございます。お手数をおかけしてしまい、こちらこそ大変申し訳ございませんでした。お手続きが完了いたしました。領収書をお渡しいたします。本日はご利用いただきありがとうございます。またのお越しを心よりお待ち申し上げております。どうかお気をつけてお帰りくださいませ。
  - EN: Thank you so much, and please allow us to apologise for the inconvenience this has caused you. Your checkout is now complete. Here is your receipt. Thank you very much for staying with us. We look forward to welcoming you back, and please have a safe journey home.
  - Feedback: Takes responsibility for the inconvenience rather than letting the guest apologise to the hotel, completes checkout graciously, and closes with a warm farewell — excellent service recovery.
- [1] ありがとうございます。手続き完了です。領収書をどうぞ。またのお越しをお待ちしております。
  - EN: Thank you. Checkout is complete. Here is your receipt. We look forward to seeing you again.
  - Feedback: Smooth and polite, but misses an apology for the inconvenience and the warmth of a more personal send-off.
- [0] では、完了です。お気をつけて。
  - EN: Right, that is done. Take care.
  - Feedback: Perfunctory and cold — after a stressful payment situation, the guest deserves a warm and apologetic close, not a dismissive sign-off.
- [0] 次回は海外でのご利用前に銀行にご連絡されることをお勧めします。
  - EN: For next time, I suggest contacting your bank before using your card abroad.
  - Feedback: Advising the guest on how to manage their bank card is unsolicited and condescending — focus on the farewell, not on lecturing.
