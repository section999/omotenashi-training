## Turn 1

**Guest:** We're not sure about the etiquette in Japanese restaurants. Any tips?
**Guest JP:** 日本のレストランでのマナーがよくわかりません。アドバイスをいただけますか？

### Choices

- [2] もちろんでございます。食事の前の「いただきます」や食後の「ごちそうさまでした」など、基本的なマナーをご説明いたします。
  - EN: Of course. I will explain basic etiquette such as saying 'itadakimasu' before eating and 'gochisousama deshita' after finishing your meal.
  - Feedback: Accurate and informative. Great cultural ambassador.
- [1] 本を読んでください。
  - EN: Please read a book about it.
  - Feedback: Redirects rather than helping personally.
- [0] 大丈夫です、心配しないで。
  - EN: It's fine, don't worry.
  - Feedback: Dismisses a genuine concern.
- [0] 日本のマナーは難しいです。
  - EN: Japanese manners are difficult.
  - Feedback: Unhelpful and discouraging.

## Turn 2

**System:** You begin explaining basic Japanese dining etiquette including greetings and customs.

**Guest:** Is tipping customary in Japan?
**Guest JP:** 日本ではチップを渡すのが一般的ですか？

### Choices

- [2] 実は、日本ではチップの習慣がございません。サービス料が含まれている場合が多く、お気持ちは笑顔でお伝えください。
  - EN: Actually, tipping is not customary in Japan. Service charge is often included, so a smile is the best way to show your appreciation.
  - Feedback: Clear, accurate cultural info with a warm touch.
- [1] チップはいりません。
  - EN: Tipping is not necessary.
  - Feedback: Correct but brief.
- [0] チップはいくらでも大丈夫です。
  - EN: Any amount of tip is fine.
  - Feedback: Incorrect. Can cause awkward situations.
- [0] レストランによります。
  - EN: It depends on the restaurant.
  - Feedback: Misleading. Tipping is generally not done in Japan.

## Turn 3

**System:** You explain that tipping is not a Japanese custom and the guest appreciates the cultural insight.

**Guest:** That's very helpful! What about removing shoes?
**Guest JP:** とても参考になりました！靴を脱ぐことについてはどうですか？

### Choices

- [2] 伝統的な旅館や畳の部屋では脱ぎますが、洋室では必要ありません。サインがある場合はそれに従ってください。
  - EN: You remove your shoes in traditional inns and tatami rooms, but it's not required in Western-style rooms. Follow any signs you see.
  - Feedback: Nuanced, accurate explanation.
- [1] 日本では靴を脱ぎます。
  - EN: In Japan, you remove your shoes.
  - Feedback: Partially correct but not nuanced.
- [0] どこでも脱いだ方がいいです。
  - EN: It's better to remove them everywhere.
  - Feedback: Overly broad and potentially incorrect.
- [0] 気にしないで大丈夫です。
  - EN: Don't worry about it, it's fine.
  - Feedback: Could lead to a cultural faux pas.
