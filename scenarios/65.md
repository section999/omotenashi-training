title: Hotel Restaurant Reservation
description: Guest wants to make a dinner reservation at the hotel's Japanese restaurant.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hi, I'd like to make a dinner reservation at your Japanese restaurant for tonight.
**Guest JP:** すみません、今夜、ホテルの日本食レストランで夕食の予約をしたいのですが。

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。本日のご夕食のご予約でございますね。何名様でいらっしゃいますか。お時間のご希望もお伺いできますでしょうか。
  - EN: Certainly. A dinner reservation for this evening — is that correct? How many guests will be dining, and do you have a preferred time?
  - Feedback: Confirms the request, asks both essential pieces of information, and does so with graceful keigo — a perfect opening.
- [1] はい、何名様で何時をご希望でしょうか？
  - EN: Yes, how many people and what time would you prefer?
  - Feedback: Efficient and correct, but skips the confirmation echo and feels slightly abrupt without a warm acknowledgment first.
- [0] 今夜は混んでいるかもしれません。
  - EN: Tonight might be busy.
  - Feedback: Flagging potential unavailability before even checking is premature and discouraging — check first, then inform.
- [0] 直接レストランにお電話ください。
  - EN: Please call the restaurant directly.
  - Feedback: Redirecting a guest to make their own call when standing at the front desk is poor service and defeats the purpose of hotel concierge.

## Turn 2

**System:** You check availability and find that a table for two is available at 7 PM tonight.

**Guest:** Is there a dress code at the restaurant?
**Guest JP:** レストランにドレスコードはありますか？

### Choices

- [2] はい、当レストランではスマートカジュアルをお願いしております。特にジーンズやサンダルはご遠慮いただいております。ご不明な点がございましたら、いつでもお申し付けくださいませ。
  - EN: Yes, our restaurant requests smart casual attire. In particular, we ask guests to refrain from wearing jeans or sandals. Please let us know if you have any questions.
  - Feedback: Specific, clear, and polite — names the standard and gives concrete examples without sounding restrictive.
- [1] スマートカジュアルをお勧めしております。
  - EN: We recommend smart casual attire.
  - Feedback: Accurate but brief — does not clarify what that means or mention what is not permitted, which may leave the guest uncertain.
- [0] 特にありません、何でも大丈夫です。
  - EN: There is no dress code — anything is fine.
  - Feedback: If a dress code exists, giving inaccurate information risks embarrassing the guest at the restaurant entrance.
- [0] レストランのスタッフに聞いてみてください。
  - EN: Please ask the restaurant staff.
  - Feedback: The front desk should know this basic information — passing the guest on for a simple question is unhelpful.

## Turn 3

**System:** You confirm the reservation at 7 PM for two guests.

**Guest:** Great, thank you! Is it possible to request a window seat?
**Guest JP:** ありがとうございます！窓際の席をリクエストすることはできますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。窓際のお席のご希望、承りました。レストランに申し伝えておきます。ご希望に添えるよう最善を尽くしますが、当日の状況によってはご要望に沿えない場合もございますことを、あらかじめご了承くださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. I have noted your preference for a window seat and will pass it on to the restaurant. We will do our best to accommodate your request, though please understand that it may not always be possible depending on the day's arrangements.
  - Feedback: Accepts the request with enthusiasm, communicates it properly, and sets honest expectations — perfectly balanced.
- [1] はい、リクエストをレストランに伝えておきます。
  - EN: Yes, I will pass on the request to the restaurant.
  - Feedback: Clear and cooperative, but does not manage the guest's expectations about whether the request can be guaranteed.
- [0] 窓際の席はお約束できません。
  - EN: We cannot promise a window seat.
  - Feedback: Jumping straight to a refusal without first acknowledging the request or committing to try sounds unwilling to help.
- [0] 当日、直接レストランに言ってください。
  - EN: Please tell the restaurant directly on the day.
  - Feedback: Passing the request along is the front desk's job — failing to relay it makes the reservation service feel incomplete.
