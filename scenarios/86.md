id: 86
title: Guest Elevator Entrapment
description: A guest calls from inside a stuck elevator that has stopped between floors.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hello?! I'm stuck in the elevator — it stopped between floors and the doors won't open. Please help!
**Guest JP:** もしもし！エレベーターに閉じ込められています！階と階の間で止まって、ドアが開きません。助けてください！

### Choices

- [2] ご連絡いただきありがとうございます。落ち着いていただいてご安心ください。今すぐ担当エンジニアとエレベーター会社に連絡いたします。お体に何かお怪我はございますか？ドアの上部または横の緊急ボタンを押していただくとお声が聞こえやすくなります。必ずそこにいてください。すぐに参ります。
  - EN: Thank you for calling — please try to stay calm, you are safe and help is on the way. I am contacting our duty engineer and the elevator company right now. Are you injured at all? If you can, please press the emergency button inside the elevator so we can stay in contact. Please stay where you are — we will reach you very soon.
  - Feedback: Immediately reassures the guest, checks for injuries, activates the correct emergency response, and gives the guest clear instructions to keep them safe — the complete and correct emergency response.
- [1] ご安心ください。エンジニアに連絡します。お怪我はありますか？
  - EN: Please stay calm. I will contact the engineer. Are you injured?
  - Feedback: Takes the right steps but is brief — in an emergency, guests need more explicit reassurance and clearer next-step information.
- [0] 少々お待ちください。確認いたします。
  - EN: Please wait a moment. I will check.
  - Feedback: Telling someone trapped in an elevator to "wait a moment" with no urgency in the response is completely inadequate and will cause panic.
- [0] エレベーターの緊急対応は管理会社が担当しております。
  - EN: Emergency elevator responses are handled by the management company.
  - Feedback: Deflecting responsibility to an external party when a guest is trapped inside the hotel's elevator is an immediate abandonment of duty of care.

## Turn 2

**System:** You alert the duty engineer and contact the elevator company; the guest asks anxiously how long it will take.

**Guest:** OK. How long is this going to take? I'm starting to feel claustrophobic.
**Guest JP:** わかりました。どのくらいかかりますか？少し閉所恐怖症になってきました。

### Choices

- [2] エンジニアがただいまお向かいしております。エレベーター会社の技術者もできる限り早急に参ります。閉所でのご不安はよく存じます。深呼吸してご安心ください。私はこのまま電話でそばにおりますので、何かあればすぐにお話しください。10分以内に対応できる見込みです。
  - EN: Our duty engineer is on their way to you right now, and the elevator technician is also en route as quickly as possible. I completely understand that being in a confined space is frightening — please try to take some slow deep breaths. I will stay on the line with you the whole time, so please talk to me whenever you need. We expect to have you out within 10 minutes.
  - Feedback: Gives a time estimate, validates the claustrophobia, offers breathing guidance, and most importantly commits to staying on the line — this personal human connection is crucial for someone experiencing anxiety in an enclosed space.
- [1] エンジニアがすぐに参ります。私はこのまま電話でおります。深呼吸してお待ちください。
  - EN: The engineer is coming right away. I will stay on the line. Please take some deep breaths while you wait.
  - Feedback: Stays on the line and offers breathing guidance, but does not give a time estimate, which is important for someone managing anxiety.
- [0] エレベーター会社の到着には時間がかかる場合があります。
  - EN: The elevator company may take some time to arrive.
  - Feedback: Suggesting a potentially long wait to someone already experiencing claustrophobia will cause immediate distress — give the best estimate and provide reassurance.
- [0] 非常口からお客様が脱出できるか確認します。
  - EN: I will check if you can exit through the emergency hatch.
  - Feedback: Suggesting a self-rescue through an emergency hatch when professional help is available is dangerous and irresponsible — keep the guest calm and wait for the engineer.

## Turn 3

**System:** The engineer arrives and releases the elevator in 8 minutes; you escort the guest personally and offer refreshments.

**Guest:** (Steps out of elevator) Oh thank goodness. That was terrifying.
**Guest JP:** （エレベーターから出て）ああ、よかった。本当に怖かったです。

### Choices

- [2] ご無事で何よりでございます。大変怖い思いをさせてしまい、誠に申し訳ございませんでした。私がお部屋までご一緒いたします。お体のご様子はいかがでしょうか。少し落ち着いていただくために、お飲み物かお菓子などをお持ちいたしましょうか。
  - EN: I am so relieved you are safe. I am truly sorry for the frightening experience you have just been through. I will personally walk you to your room. How are you feeling? May I bring you something to drink, or a small snack to help you feel more settled?
  - Feedback: Expresses genuine relief, takes personal responsibility for the experience, offers a personal escort, checks on the guest's wellbeing, and offers a thoughtful comfort gesture — everything the moment calls for.
- [1] ご無事でよかったです。お部屋までご一緒しましょう。何かお持ちしましょうか。
  - EN: I am glad you are safe. Let me walk you to your room. Can I bring you anything?
  - Feedback: Warm and practical, but could be more apologetic for the frightening experience and more specific about the refreshment offer.
- [0] エレベーターは現在点検中です。ご利用にはしばらくお時間がかかります。
  - EN: The elevator is currently being inspected. It will be some time before it can be used again.
  - Feedback: Leading with operational information rather than care for the guest who has just been through a frightening ordeal is entirely the wrong priority.
- [0] 以後はお気をつけください。
  - EN: Please be careful in future.
  - Feedback: Implying the guest could have prevented being trapped in a faulty elevator is inappropriate and unkind — this was entirely the hotel's responsibility.
