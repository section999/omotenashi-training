## Turn 1

**System:** A guest approaches the concierge desk the evening before departure to arrange transport to the airport.

**Guest:** I need a car to the airport tomorrow morning. My flight leaves at nine.
**Guest JP:** 明日の朝、空港まで車をお願いしたいのですが。フライトが9時なんです。

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。9時のフライトでございますね。空港まではおよそ1時間かかりますので、7時ごろのご出発がよろしいかと存じます。タクシーとリムジンバス、どちらをご希望でしょうか？
  - EN: Certainly. Your flight is at 9 AM. As it takes approximately one hour to the airport, we recommend departing around 7 AM. Would you prefer a private taxi or the airport limousine bus?
  - Feedback: Calculates a realistic departure time and offers clear options — exactly what a skilled concierge does.
- [1] はい、空港への送迎を手配いたします。ご希望の出発時刻はございますか？
  - EN: Yes, I will arrange transport to the airport. What time would you like to depart?
  - Feedback: Correct approach — proactively suggesting a departure time based on the flight would be more helpful.
- [0] タクシーはご自身でお呼びいただいたほうが早いかと思います。
  - EN: It may be quicker if you call a taxi yourself.
  - Feedback: Concierge service means handling this for the guest — never redirect the task back to them.
- [0] 空港バスのご利用もございます。
  - EN: The airport bus is also available.
  - Feedback: Useful to mention, but ask about the guest's preference first rather than jumping to one option.

## Turn 2

**System:** Guest chooses a private taxi and mentions they have four large suitcases.

**Guest:** I'll take a taxi, please. I have four large suitcases — will they all fit?
**Guest JP:** タクシーでお願いします。大きいスーツケースが4つあるのですが、全部乗りますか？

### Choices

- [2] ご安心ください。大型スーツケース4個でしたら、ジャンボタクシーのご手配が必要でございます。追加料金は発生いたしますが、お荷物を全てゆとりをもってお乗せできます。ご手配してよろしいでしょうか？
  - EN: Please do not worry. For four large suitcases, we will need to arrange a jumbo taxi. There will be an additional charge, but all your luggage will fit comfortably. Shall I go ahead and book it?
  - Feedback: Honest about the extra charge, confirms the solution, and seeks the guest's approval — transparent and professional.
- [1] 大型のお荷物が多い場合は、大きな車をご手配いたします。
  - EN: For a large amount of luggage, we can arrange a larger vehicle.
  - Feedback: Correct approach — mentioning the additional charge upfront and asking for confirmation is equally important.
- [0] 通常のタクシーでは乗り切らない場合がございます。
  - EN: A standard taxi may not be able to fit everything.
  - Feedback: Identifies the problem but does not offer a solution — propose the jumbo taxi immediately.
- [0] お荷物はベルボーイがまとめてくれますのでご安心ください。
  - EN: Please do not worry — the bellboy will help gather your luggage.
  - Feedback: Carrying assistance is a separate matter from vehicle capacity — address the space issue first.

## Turn 3

**System:** The jumbo taxi is confirmed for 7:00 AM. Guest asks about how to pay for the taxi.

**Guest:** Should I pay the hotel, or do I pay the driver when I get to the airport?
**Guest JP:** タクシー代はホテルで払うんですか？それとも空港に着いてから運転手に払うんでしょうか？

### Choices

- [2] タクシー代は到着時に運転手へ直接お支払いいただきます。現金もクレジットカードもご利用いただけます。なお、ベルスタッフが7時にお部屋へお荷物をお取りに参りますので、ご安心くださいませ。
  - EN: Payment is made directly to the driver upon arrival. Both cash and credit cards are accepted. Our bell staff will come to your room at seven to collect your luggage, so please do not worry.
  - Feedback: Clear on payment method, mentions card acceptance, and adds the helpful bellhop detail — a complete and reassuring answer.
- [1] 運転手への直接お支払いとなります。現金とカードがご利用いただけます。
  - EN: Payment is made directly to the driver. Cash and cards are both accepted.
  - Feedback: Correct and clear — mentioning the bellhop luggage service would make this response more complete.
- [0] 詳しい支払い方法は運転手にご確認ください。
  - EN: Please check the payment details with the driver.
  - Feedback: The concierge should know the standard payment procedure — do not defer to the driver for basic information.
- [0] ホテルの精算に含めることもできます。
  - EN: We can also include it in your hotel bill.
  - Feedback: Only say this if it is actually possible — if it is not the hotel's policy, stating it will create confusion and disappointment.
