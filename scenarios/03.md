## Turn 1

**Guest:** Table for two, please.
**Guest JP:** 2名でお願いします。

### Choices

- [2] 2名様でございますね。こちらへどうぞ。ご案内いたします。
  - EN: A table for two, is that right? Right this way. I will show you to your seat.
  - Feedback: Perfect confirmation and smooth guidance.
- [1] はい、2人ですね。どうぞ。
  - EN: Yes, two people. Please go ahead.
  - Feedback: Correct but lacks polite keigo.
- [0] そこに座ってください。
  - EN: Please sit there.
  - Feedback: Too abrupt. Always guide guests with care.
- [0] 少し待ってください。
  - EN: Please wait a moment.
  - Feedback: Don't make guests wait without explanation.

## Turn 2

**System:** You guide the guests to their table and hand them menus.

**Guest:** What do you recommend today?
**Guest JP:** 本日のおすすめは何ですか？

### Choices

- [2] 本日のおすすめは季節の野菜を使ったリゾットでございます。大変好評いただいております。
  - EN: Today's recommendation is a risotto made with seasonal vegetables. It has been very well received.
  - Feedback: Confident recommendation with a reason.
- [1] リゾットがおいしいです。
  - EN: The risotto is delicious.
  - Feedback: Gives info but lacks polite language.
- [0] メニューを見てください。
  - EN: Please look at the menu.
  - Feedback: Dismissive. Guests expect recommendations.
- [0] 全部おいしいです。
  - EN: Everything is delicious.
  - Feedback: Unhelpful non-answer.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guests look over the menu while you describe the day's specials.

**Guest:** I'll have the risotto and a glass of white wine.
**Guest JP:** リゾットと白ワインをグラスで一杯お願いします。

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。リゾットと白ワインでございますね。少々お待ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. That will be the risotto and a glass of white wine. Please wait just a moment.
  - Feedback: Perfect order confirmation with polite waiting phrase.
- [1] はい、分かりました。
  - EN: Yes, understood.
  - Feedback: Correct but doesn't confirm the order.
- [0] ワインは何がいいですか？
  - EN: What kind of wine would you like?
  - Feedback: Should confirm the type they asked for first.
- [0] 少し時間かかります。
  - EN: It will take a little time.
  - Feedback: Vague and unprofessional without context.
