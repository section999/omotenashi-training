title: Room Temperature Issue
description: Guest calls to say the air conditioning is not working properly — the room is too hot.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** The room is really hot and the AC doesn't seem to be working. Can you do something about it?
**Guest JP:** 部屋がすごく暑くて、エアコンが動いていないみたいなんですが、どうにかなりますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。お部屋が暑くなっているとのこと、大変申し訳ございません。ただちにメンテナンス担当者をお伺いさせますので、少々お待ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. I am very sorry to hear that your room is uncomfortably hot. I will send our maintenance staff to you right away — please bear with us for a moment.
  - Feedback: Excellent — sincere apology, immediate action, and a polite request for patience all in one response.
- [1] 申し訳ございません。メンテナンスの者をお送りします。
  - EN: I apologise. I will send someone from maintenance.
  - Feedback: Polite and adequate, but lacks a confirmation echo of the problem and offers no sense of urgency.
- [0] エアコンの設定温度を確認してみてください。
  - EN: Please check the temperature setting on the air conditioner.
  - Feedback: Deflects responsibility onto the guest without offering direct assistance — dismissive.
- [0] 今、他のお客様からも連絡が来ています。
  - EN: We are currently receiving calls from other guests as well.
  - Feedback: Sharing operational issues with the guest is inappropriate and does nothing to resolve their problem.

## Turn 2

**System:** You apologise and confirm that maintenance will be sent to the room immediately.

**Guest:** OK, but how long will it take?
**Guest JP:** わかりました。でも、どのくらい時間がかかりますか？

### Choices

- [2] おおよそ10分以内にお伺いできる予定でございます。お待たせしてしまい、誠に申し訳ございません。もしよろしければ、お待ちの間、ロビーのラウンジをご利用くださいませ。
  - EN: We expect someone to arrive within about 10 minutes. We are truly sorry to keep you waiting. If you wish, please feel free to use our lobby lounge while you wait.
  - Feedback: Gives a precise estimate, adds a genuine apology, and offers a thoughtful alternative — exemplary hospitality.
- [1] 10分ほどかと存じます。少々お待ちいただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: I believe it will be around 10 minutes. Would you mind waiting a little?
  - Feedback: Appropriately humble and polite, but misses the chance to offer the guest somewhere cooler to wait.
- [0] すぐに行かせます。
  - EN: We will send someone right away.
  - Feedback: Vague — "right away" without a time estimate is not reassuring when a guest is uncomfortable.
- [0] メンテナンスのスケジュールによります。
  - EN: It depends on the maintenance schedule.
  - Feedback: Sounds bureaucratic and indifferent; the guest is hot and needs a concrete answer.

## Turn 3

**System:** Maintenance arrives and fixes the air conditioning unit successfully.

**Guest:** Oh, it's working now. Thank you.
**Guest JP:** あ、直りました。ありがとうございます。

### Choices

- [2] ご不便をおかけいたしまして、誠に申し訳ございませんでした。エアコンが回復いたしましたこと、安心いたしました。他に何かお気づきの点やご要望がございましたら、いつでもお申し付けくださいませ。
  - EN: We sincerely apologise for the inconvenience caused. I am relieved to hear the air conditioning is working again. Please do not hesitate to let us know if you notice anything else or if there is anything you need.
  - Feedback: Closes the issue with a genuine apology, expresses care, and proactively invites further requests — a complete and warm response.
- [1] 直ってよかったです。何かあればまたご連絡ください。
  - EN: I am glad it is fixed. Please call us again if anything comes up.
  - Feedback: Friendly and clear, but the apology for the inconvenience is missing and the phrasing is less formal than ideal.
- [0] はい、承知しました。
  - EN: Yes, understood.
  - Feedback: A bare acknowledgment with no apology or follow-up offer — fails to close the interaction properly.
- [0] 最初からそうなっていれば良かったのですが。
  - EN: It would have been better if it had been working from the start.
  - Feedback: An awkward and unnecessary remark that highlights the failure rather than reassuring the guest.
