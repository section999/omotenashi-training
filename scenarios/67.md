id: 67
title: Arranging a Taxi
description: Guest needs a taxi arranged for an early morning airport transfer.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Could you arrange a taxi for me to the airport at 5:30 tomorrow morning?
**Guest JP:** 明日の朝5時半に空港まで、タクシーを手配してもらえますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。明日の午前5時半、空港へのタクシーのご手配でございますね。承りました。ご利用になる空港と、フライトのご出発時刻をお伺いできますでしょうか。
  - EN: Certainly. A taxi to the airport at 5:30 AM tomorrow morning — is that correct? Understood. May I ask which airport you are travelling to and your flight departure time?
  - Feedback: Confirms the request precisely, uses 承りました, and asks for the right details to ensure the guest reaches their flight on time.
- [1] はい、5時半ですね。空港はどちらですか？
  - EN: Yes, 5:30 AM. Which airport?
  - Feedback: Gets the necessary information but skips the warm confirmation echo and is noticeably terse.
- [0] タクシーは外で自分で拾えますよ。
  - EN: You can hail a taxi outside yourself.
  - Feedback: Telling the guest to arrange their own taxi when they specifically asked for hotel assistance is a failure of service.
- [0] その時間は難しいかもしれません。
  - EN: That time might be difficult.
  - Feedback: Expressing doubt before checking is premature — confirm first, then communicate any issues if they exist.

## Turn 2

**System:** You confirm the taxi booking and note the guest's flight details for reference.

**Guest:** Thanks. And how long does it take to get to the airport from here?
**Guest JP:** ありがとうございます。ここから空港まで、どのくらい時間がかかりますか？

### Choices

- [2] 通常、当ホテルから空港までは約40分でございます。早朝の5時半出発であれば交通渋滞の心配はほとんどございませんが、余裕を持って6時15分のフライトには十分間に合う予定でございます。どうぞご安心くださいませ。
  - EN: Under normal conditions, it takes approximately 40 minutes from the hotel to the airport. Departing at 5:30 AM, there is very little risk of traffic, and you should be in good time for your 6:15 AM flight. Please rest assured.
  - Feedback: Gives a specific time, factors in the early hour, connects it to the guest's flight details, and closes reassuringly — excellent.
- [1] 通常40分ほどかかります。朝早いので渋滞はないと思います。
  - EN: It usually takes about 40 minutes. Since it is early morning, there should not be any traffic.
  - Feedback: Informative and reassuring, but does not reference the guest's flight time to make the advice feel personalised.
- [0] 場合によって違います。
  - EN: It depends on the situation.
  - Feedback: Vague and unhelpful — the guest needs a concrete estimate to plan their morning.
- [0] 早めに出た方がいいですよ。
  - EN: You should leave a bit early.
  - Feedback: Unsolicited advice without giving the actual travel time first does not answer the question and sounds presumptuous.

## Turn 3

**System:** You confirm the travel time and, as an extra gesture, offer to arrange a wake-up call for the guest.

**Guest:** A wake-up call sounds great. Thank you — goodnight!
**Guest JP:** モーニングコールをお願いします。ありがとう、おやすみなさい！

### Choices

- [2] ありがとうございます。では、明朝4時45分にモーニングコールをご用意いたします。タクシーのご出発まで余裕を持ってお起きいただけるかと存じます。どうぞゆっくりおやすみくださいませ。明日、安全なご旅行をお祈り申し上げます。
  - EN: Thank you. I will arrange a wake-up call for 4:45 AM tomorrow morning, giving you plenty of time before your taxi departs. Please enjoy a restful night's sleep. We wish you a safe journey tomorrow.
  - Feedback: Sets a thoughtful wake-up time with the taxi in mind, uses 存じます naturally, and sends the guest off with a warm farewell — truly excellent.
- [1] かしこまりました。4時45分にモーニングコールいたします。おやすみなさいませ。
  - EN: Certainly. We will call you at 4:45 AM. Goodnight.
  - Feedback: Efficient and polite, but does not explain why 4:45 was chosen or wish the guest a good journey.
- [0] わかりました、モーニングコールします。
  - EN: Understood, we will give you a wake-up call.
  - Feedback: Bare acknowledgment with no time specified, no farewell, and no warmth — unsuitable for a hotel guest heading to bed.
- [0] 何時に起こせばいいですか？
  - EN: What time would you like to be woken up?
  - Feedback: The staff should use their knowledge of the taxi time to suggest an appropriate wake-up time rather than putting the decision back on the guest.
