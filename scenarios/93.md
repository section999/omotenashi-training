id: 93
title: Baby Crib and Infant Amenity Request
description: A guest calls housekeeping to request a baby crib for their infant, and staff offers additional baby amenities.
---

## Turn 1

**System:** A guest calls the housekeeping line asking for a baby crib to be brought to their room. You need to confirm availability and gather necessary details.

**Guest:** Hi, we have an 8-month-old baby with us and we'd like to request a crib for the room, please.
**Guest JP:** もしもし、8ヶ月の赤ちゃんがいるので、お部屋にベビーベッドをお願いしたいのですが。

### Choices

- [2] 「かしこまりました。ベビーベッドのご用意が可能でございます。お部屋番号をお伺いできますでしょうか？また、念のためお子様の月齢を確認させていただいてもよろしいでしょうか？」
  - EN: Certainly. We are able to provide a baby crib. May I have your room number? Also, just to confirm, may I ask the baby's age in months?
  - Feedback: This is the ideal response. It immediately confirms availability (which reassures the guest), then gathers the two essential pieces of information — room number for delivery and baby's age to ensure the right crib size and safety standard is provided.
- [1] 「はい、少々お待ちください。空き状況を確認いたします。」
  - EN: Yes, please hold a moment while I check availability.
  - Feedback: Acceptable if availability genuinely needs to be verified, but making the guest wait before even acknowledging the request feels unnecessarily uncertain. Ideally, front-line staff should know standard amenity availability.
- [0] 「ベビーベッドはレンタル料金が別途かかりますが、よろしいですか？」
  - EN: There will be a separate rental charge for the baby crib — is that alright?
  - Feedback: Leading with cost information before confirming willingness to help or asking for room details is off-putting. In a 5-star hotel, baby cribs are almost universally a complimentary amenity. Mentioning a charge without basis may create a false impression.
- [0] 「ベビーベッドの持ち込みは安全上の理由でお断りしております。」
  - EN: We do not allow personally brought baby cribs for safety reasons.
  - Feedback: The guest is not bringing their own crib — they are requesting the hotel's crib. This response is based on a misunderstanding of the request and is entirely inappropriate.

## Turn 2

**System:** The guest confirms their room number (Room 815) and confirms the baby is 8 months old. Now offer the crib delivery and introduce additional baby amenities available.

**Guest:** We're in Room 815. She's 8 months old — still not walking yet but she's very active!
**Guest JP:** 815号室です。生後8ヶ月で、まだ歩けませんがとても活発なんです！

### Choices

- [2] 「815号室のお客様、かしこまりました。生後8ヶ月でいらっしゃるのですね。ベビーベッドを30分以内にお届けいたします。また、バスチェアや消毒済みの離乳食用食器、赤ちゃん用アメニティセットもご用意できますが、ご利用になりますか？」
  - EN: Understood, Room 815. Eight months old — noted. We will deliver the crib within 30 minutes. We also have a bath chair, sterilized weaning tableware, and a baby amenity kit available — would you like any of those as well?
  - Feedback: This is the ideal response. The room number is confirmed, a delivery timeframe is given, the baby's age is acknowledged warmly, and additional relevant amenities are proactively offered — showing genuine anticipation of a family's needs.
- [1] 「815号室ですね、ありがとうございます。ベビーベッドをお届けします。何か他にご入用のものはございますか？」
  - EN: Room 815, thank you. We will bring the crib. Is there anything else you need?
  - Feedback: The delivery is confirmed and a general offer of help is made, but asking 「何か他にご入用のものはございますか？」 puts the burden on the guest to know what's available. Proactively mentioning specific baby amenities is far more helpful.
- [0] 「8ヶ月ですと、まだベビーベッドが必要な月齢ですね。お届けします。」
  - EN: At 8 months, that is still an age where a crib is needed. We will bring it.
  - Feedback: Stating the obvious about the baby's developmental stage is unnecessary and slightly patronizing. Get to the service — confirm the delivery and offer additional help without commentary.
- [0] 「少し時間がかかりますので、本日中にお届けするよう手配いたします。」
  - EN: It will take some time, so we will arrange to deliver it today.
  - Feedback: "Today" is far too vague a timeframe for a family with an infant. Parents need to plan — a specific timeframe like "within 30 minutes" is necessary to be genuinely useful.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guest is happy and asks about the bath chair — specifically whether the hotel's bathroom is safe for bathing an 8-month-old and whether staff can help set it up.

**Guest:** Oh, a bath chair would be wonderful! Is the bathroom suitable for bathing a baby? And could someone help set it up?
**Guest JP:** まあ、バスチェアがあるんですね！お風呂は赤ちゃんを入れても大丈夫ですか？設置もお願いできますか？

### Choices

- [2] 「はい、バスチェアはベビーベッドと一緒にお持ちし、スタッフがお部屋でご使用方法をご説明しながら設置いたします。バスタブのサイズも赤ちゃんのご入浴に十分対応しており、お湯の温度調節もしやすい設計になっております。ご不明な点がございましたら、いつでもハウスキーピングへお申し付けください。」
  - EN: Yes, we will bring the bath chair together with the crib, and a staff member will explain how to use it and set it up in your room. Our bathtub size is well suited for bathing a baby, and the water temperature is easy to adjust. Please do not hesitate to call housekeeping if you have any questions.
  - Feedback: Perfect. The staff commits to bringing both items together, offers in-room setup assistance, reassures the guest about bathroom suitability, and gives an open invitation for further help — a complete and caring response.
- [1] 「バスチェアをお届けする際に使い方をご説明します。バスタブは通常のサイズですので、赤ちゃんにもお使いいただけます。」
  - EN: We will explain how to use the bath chair when we deliver it. The bathtub is standard size and can be used for your baby.
  - Feedback: The basic information is covered, but the response is brief and matter-of-fact. There is no warmth or reassurance around safety, and no invitation for further assistance — it feels transactional rather than caring.
- [0] 「バスタブは大人用なので、赤ちゃんの入浴には適さない場合があります。洗面台のご利用をお勧めします。」
  - EN: The bathtub is designed for adults, so it may not be suitable for bathing a baby. We suggest using the washbasin.
  - Feedback: Recommending a washbasin to bathe an 8-month-old is impractical and shows a lack of understanding of infant care. This response undermines the guest's confidence and offers a poor substitute rather than proper assistance.
- [0] 「設置はお客様ご自身でお願いいたします。説明書が同封されております。」
  - EN: Please set it up yourself. An instruction manual is included.
  - Feedback: A guest with an 8-month-old infant who specifically asks for setup help should not be told to do it themselves. This is a cold and unhelpful response that fails the most basic standard of hospitality service.
