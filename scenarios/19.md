---
id: 19
title: Medical Assistance
description: A guest needs urgent medical help.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** My husband feels dizzy and nauseous. We're worried.
**Guest JP:** 夫がめまいと吐き気がしています。心配です。

### Choices

- [2] ご心配のことと存じます。すぐに救急の手配と、館内のAEDをご用意いたします。今すぐ参ります。
  - EN: I understand you are worried. I will arrange emergency services and prepare the in-hotel AED immediately. I will be right there.
  - Feedback: Immediate, decisive action. Safety first.
- [1] 病院に行ってください。
  - EN: Please go to the hospital.
  - Feedback: Correct but the guest needs immediate in-hotel help.
- [0] 少し休んだら大丈夫ですよ。
  - EN: It will be fine after a little rest.
  - Feedback: Never dismiss medical symptoms casually.
- [0] フロントに来てください。
  - EN: Please come to the front desk.
  - Feedback: Ill guest should not be asked to move.

## Turn 2

**System:** You respond urgently, prepare to dispatch help, and head toward the guest's room.

**Guest:** Should we call an ambulance?
**Guest JP:** 救急車を呼んだ方がいいですか？

### Choices

- [2] はい、ただちに119番に連絡いたします。お部屋番号を教えていただけますか？
  - EN: Yes, I will contact emergency services at 119 immediately. Could you give me your room number?
  - Feedback: Decisive. Takes control and asks for location.
- [1] ご安心ください。すぐに確認して対応いたします。
  - EN: Please don't worry. I will check and respond right away.
  - Feedback: Reassuring, but should ask about calling an ambulance more directly.
- [0] 自分で電話してください。
  - EN: Please call yourself.
  - Feedback: Staff must assist in emergencies. Never refuse.
- [0] もう少し様子を見ましょう。
  - EN: Let's wait and see for a little while.
  - Feedback: Dangerous advice for medical situations.

## Turn 3

**System:** You call emergency services and coordinate the response while staying with the guest.

**Guest:** Thank you so much. We really appreciate your help.
**Guest JP:** 本当にありがとうございます。大変助かりました。

### Choices

- [2] お役に立てて幸いです。どうかご主人様が早くご回復なさいますよう、心よりお祈り申し上げます。
  - EN: I am glad we could help. I sincerely hope your husband makes a swift recovery.
  - Feedback: Genuine, warm, and appropriately heartfelt.
- [1] 大丈夫ですか？
  - EN: Are you alright?
  - Feedback: Caring question but comes after the thanks.
- [0] 仕事ですから。
  - EN: It's my job.
  - Feedback: Sounds cold. Show genuine concern.
- [0] それほどでもないです。
  - EN: It was nothing, really.
  - Feedback: Self-deprecating in a way that minimizes the impact.
