id: 91
title: Formal Receipt Request After Checkout
description: A guest requests an official receipt for business expenses after completing checkout.
---

## Turn 1

**System:** A guest has just finished checking out at the front desk and asks for a formal receipt (領収書) for their stay, which will be submitted as a business expense.

**Guest:** Excuse me, I need an official receipt for my stay — it's for business expenses. Can you make one out?
**Guest JP:** すみません、今回の宿泊代を経費で落とすので、領収書をいただけますか？

### Choices

- [2] 「かしこまりました。領収書をお作りいたします。宛名はどのようにいたしましょうか？」
  - EN: Certainly. I will prepare a formal receipt for you. How shall I make out the addressee name?
  - Feedback: This is the ideal response — it immediately confirms the request, commits to action, and asks the most critical piece of information (宛名) in a single, efficient keigo sentence.
- [1] 「はい、領収書ですね。お名前を教えていただけますか？」
  - EN: Yes, a receipt. Could you tell me your name?
  - Feedback: Acceptable, but asking for お名前 (personal name) is slightly less precise than asking for 宛名 (addressee name), which for business use may be a company name rather than a personal name.
- [0] 「領収書はチェックアウトの前にお申し付けいただく必要がございます。」
  - EN: You need to request a receipt before checkout.
  - Feedback: This is incorrect and unhelpful. A hotel cannot refuse to issue a receipt after checkout. It creates unnecessary conflict and is factually wrong practice for a 5-star hotel.
- [0] 「レシートはお持ちですか？それを使っていただけますか？」
  - EN: Do you have your receipt? Could you use that?
  - Feedback: A printed machine receipt (レシート) and a formal 領収書 are legally distinct documents in Japan. Deflecting the guest in this way shows ignorance of Japanese business practice.

## Turn 2

**System:** The guest has provided the addressee name — their company, "株式会社グローバルテック". Staff now needs to confirm the total amount and ask how the guest would like to receive the receipt.

**Guest:** The company name is Global Tech Co., Ltd. — "Kabushiki Kaisha Global Tech." The total was ¥48,000, right?
**Guest JP:** 宛名は「株式会社グローバルテック」でお願いします。金額は48,000円でよろしいでしょうか？

### Choices

- [2] 「株式会社グローバルテック様、承知いたしました。ご宿泊代金の合計は48,000円でございます。領収書はPDFでメールにてお送りすることも可能でございますが、いかがでしょうか？」
  - EN: Understood, Global Tech Co., Ltd. The total accommodation charge is ¥48,000. We can also send the receipt as a PDF by email — would that be convenient for you?
  - Feedback: Ideal response. It echoes the company name with 様 to confirm, verifies the amount clearly, and proactively offers the PDF email option, which is exactly what a modern 5-star hotel should do.
- [1] 「はい、48,000円で間違いございません。紙の領収書をお渡しします。」
  - EN: Yes, ¥48,000 is correct. I will give you a paper receipt.
  - Feedback: Acceptable — the amount is confirmed and a receipt will be issued. However, not offering the PDF email option misses a valuable service touch that business travelers appreciate.
- [0] 「金額については経理に確認が必要ですので、少々お待ちください。」
  - EN: We need to check the amount with accounting, so please wait a moment.
  - Feedback: The front desk agent has access to the guest's folio and should be able to confirm the total immediately. Escalating to accounting for a routine amount confirmation is unnecessarily slow and creates doubt in the guest's mind.
- [0] 「消費税は含まれておりますが、サービス料は別です。」
  - EN: That includes consumption tax, but service charge is separate.
  - Feedback: While tax information may be relevant, leading with an unsolicited and confusing breakdown when the guest simply asked to confirm ¥48,000 is unhelpful. Confirm the total first before explaining itemization.

## Turn 3

**System:** The guest accepts the PDF option and provides their email address. Staff must confirm the email, state when it will be sent, and close the interaction warmly.

**Guest:** Yes, PDF by email would be perfect. My address is tanaka@globaltech.co.jp.
**Guest JP:** はい、PDFでメールをいただけると助かります。アドレスは tanaka@globaltech.co.jp です。

### Choices

- [2] 「tanaka@globaltech.co.jp 様ですね、かしこまりました。本日中にご登録のメールアドレスへ領収書PDFをお送りいたします。この度はご宿泊いただきまして、誠にありがとうございました。またのお越しをお待ち申し上げております。」
  - EN: That is tanaka@globaltech.co.jp — understood. We will send the receipt PDF to that address today. Thank you sincerely for your stay. We look forward to welcoming you again.
  - Feedback: Perfect closing. The email is read back to confirm accuracy, a clear send timeline is given, and the farewell is warm and complete — exactly what a 5-star hotel farewell should sound like.
- [1] 「わかりました。後ほどメールをお送りします。またお越しください。」
  - EN: Understood. We will send an email later. Please come again.
  - Feedback: The task is completed but the language is too casual. 「わかりました」 and 「後ほど」 without a timeframe are vague, and the farewell lacks the warmth expected of luxury hospitality.
- [0] 「メールアドレスをこちらの用紙にご記入いただけますか？」
  - EN: Could you write your email address on this form?
  - Feedback: The guest just verbally provided the address. Asking them to write it down again after they've spoken it is redundant and slightly dismissive. Simply read it back and confirm.
- [0] 「送信まで3営業日かかる場合がございます。」
  - EN: It may take up to 3 business days to send.
  - Feedback: A 3-day delay for a PDF receipt email is completely unacceptable in a modern hotel context. This would seriously disappoint a business traveler. Same-day delivery is the standard.
