title: Guest Arriving with Guide Dog
description: A visually impaired guest arrives with their guide dog and needs correct, welcoming assistance.
---

## Turn 1

**System:** A guest with a visual impairment approaches the front desk with a guide dog and checks whether it is permitted.

**Guest:** I have a guide dog with me. Is that all right to bring inside?
**Guest JP:** 盲導犬を連れておりますが、ご一緒してもよろしいでしょうか？

### Choices

- [2] もちろんでございます。盲導犬のご同伴は法律により受け入れが義務付けられております。安心してお入りください。お部屋のご用意もできております。
  - EN: Of course. Under Japanese law, guide dogs must be accommodated. Please come right in. Your room is ready for you.
  - Feedback: Immediately reassures the guest, references the legal basis, and moves straight to check-in — excellent.
- [1] はい、盲導犬でしたら問題ございません。どうぞお入りください。
  - EN: Yes, guide dogs are perfectly fine. Please come in.
  - Feedback: Welcoming and appropriate — mentioning the legal right to enter would provide added reassurance.
- [0] ペットはご遠慮いただいております。
  - EN: Pets are not permitted.
  - Feedback: Critically incorrect. Guide dogs are service animals protected by the 身体障害者補助犬法 and must never be refused.
- [0] 上の者に確認してまいります。少々お待ちください。
  - EN: I will check with my supervisor. One moment please.
  - Feedback: Unacceptable delay — the answer is unambiguous. Never make a guest with a guide dog wait for approval.

## Turn 2

**System:** Guest has checked in and asks where the dog can be taken outside to relieve herself.

**Guest:** Is there an area outside where my dog can go to the toilet?
**Guest JP:** 犬がトイレできるような場所は、外にありますか？

### Choices

- [2] はい、ホテル裏手の緑地エリアをご利用いただけます。地図をお渡しいたします。後片付け用の袋もフロントにてご用意しておりますので、お申し付けください。
  - EN: Yes, please use the green area behind the hotel. I will give you a map. We also have waste bags available at the front desk — just let us know.
  - Feedback: Specific location, a map, and waste bags — practical and genuinely considerate.
- [1] ホテルの外に公園がございます。フロントで地図をご用意できます。
  - EN: There is a park outside the hotel. We can provide a map at the front desk.
  - Feedback: Helpful, though the hotel's own green area is more convenient — offer that first before suggesting the park.
- [0] ホテル敷地内での犬のトイレはご遠慮ください。
  - EN: Please refrain from letting the dog relieve herself on hotel grounds.
  - Feedback: Refusing without an alternative is unhospitable — always direct the guest to an appropriate designated area.
- [0] そちらはご自身でお探しいただくことになります。
  - EN: You will need to find a suitable place yourself.
  - Feedback: Leaving a visually impaired guest to navigate unfamiliar surroundings alone is entirely unacceptable.

## Turn 3

**System:** Guest asks whether the dog can remain in the room unattended while they dine at the hotel restaurant.

**Guest:** Can my dog stay in the room by herself while I have dinner downstairs?
**Guest JP:** レストランで食事をしている間、犬を1頭でお部屋に残しておいても大丈夫ですか？

### Choices

- [2] はい、お部屋でお留守番いただくことは可能でございます。ご心配でしたら、お食事中にスタッフが様子を見に参ることもできますので、お申し付けくださいませ。
  - EN: Yes, it is perfectly fine for your dog to stay in the room. If you are concerned, our staff can also check in on her while you are dining — please do let us know.
  - Feedback: Reassures the guest and proactively offers a welfare check — thoughtful and inclusive hospitality.
- [1] はい、お部屋でお待ちいただいて問題ございません。
  - EN: Yes, it is fine for the dog to wait in the room.
  - Feedback: Correct and direct — offering to check on the dog while the guest is away would make this response more caring.
- [0] 鳴き声が他のお客様のご迷惑になる場合がございます。
  - EN: The dog's barking may disturb other guests.
  - Feedback: Unsolicited concern — answer the guest's question first before raising any caveats.
- [0] レストランへのご同伴については別途ご相談ください。
  - EN: Please enquire separately about bringing the dog to the restaurant.
  - Feedback: The guest did not ask about bringing the dog to the restaurant — read the question carefully before responding.
