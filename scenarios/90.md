id: 90
title: Corporate Account Processing Failure
description: A business guest at checkout finds their corporate billing account is not in the system, leaving a 180,000 yen bill unresolved.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hi, I'm checking out. I'm on a corporate account — here's the account number for Nakamura Industries.
**Guest JP:** チェックアウトをお願いします。法人アカウントです。中村産業のアカウント番号はこちらです。

### Choices

- [2] ありがとうございます。中村産業様のアカウントをただいまシステムで確認いたします。少々お待ちくださいませ。……大変申し上げにくいのですが、現時点でこちらのアカウントがシステムに登録されていない状況でございます。ご不便をおかけし、誠に申し訳ございません。直ちに担当部署に確認いたします。
  - EN: Thank you. I am checking the Nakamura Industries account in our system right now — please bear with me for a moment. I am very sorry to say that at this time the account does not appear to be registered in our system. I sincerely apologise for this difficulty. I am contacting our reservations team immediately to investigate.
  - Feedback: Apologises sincerely for the administrative failure, does not imply any fault on the guest's part, and immediately escalates to the right team — the correct and professional first response.
- [1] 確認いたします。……申し訳ございませんが、アカウントが見当たりません。担当に確認いたします。
  - EN: I will check. I am sorry, but the account cannot be found. I will contact the relevant team.
  - Feedback: Covers the key steps but lacks a sincere enough apology for an error that places the guest in a difficult checkout situation.
- [0] このアカウント番号は当ホテルでは受け付けられません。
  - EN: This account number is not accepted at our hotel.
  - Feedback: Flatly rejecting the account without investigating is inappropriate — the guest has a legitimate corporate relationship that should be looked into before any conclusion is drawn.
- [0] お支払いは現金またはカードでお願いします。
  - EN: Please pay by cash or card.
  - Feedback: Immediately reverting to alternative payment without any investigation or apology is dismissive and ignores the guest's established corporate arrangement.

## Turn 2

**System:** You contact the reservations team and discover the corporate contract was not yet set up in the system; the guest is frustrated and mentions they have a flight in 2 hours.

**Guest:** This is really inconvenient. I've used this account at other hotels in your chain before. I have a flight in two hours.
**Guest JP:** これは本当に不便ですね。このアカウントは御社グループの他のホテルでも使ったことがあります。2時間後にフライトがあります。

### Choices

- [2] 大変ご不便をおかけしております。誠に申し訳ございません。担当部署より確認が取れました。法人契約のシステムへの登録が完了していなかったことが原因と判明いたしました。これは完全に当ホテルの手続き上の不備でございます。フライトまでのお時間を考慮し、今回はお客様個人のカードにてお支払いいただき、法人アカウントへの全額ご請求を3営業日以内に処理させていただく形でいかがでしょうか。書面にてご確認いただけますよう準備いたします。
  - EN: I am deeply sorry for this serious inconvenience — it is entirely our fault. Our reservations team has confirmed that the corporate contract had not yet been registered in the system, which is a failure on our end. Given your flight time, may I suggest that you pay by personal card today, and we will process the full charge back to your corporate account within three business days? I will prepare written confirmation of this arrangement for you now.
  - Feedback: Takes full responsibility, explains the cause clearly, proposes a practical solution that respects the guest's time pressure, and offers written confirmation — comprehensive service recovery under pressure.
- [1] こちらのミスで大変申し訳ございません。個人カードでお支払いいただき、後日法人アカウントに請求いたします。
  - EN: I am very sorry for our error. Please pay by personal card today and we will charge the corporate account later.
  - Feedback: Apologises and proposes the right solution, but does not mention written confirmation or a specific timeline, which is essential for a business guest.
- [0] 法人契約についてはご担当者様に確認していただく必要があります。
  - EN: You will need to check with your corporate account manager.
  - Feedback: Pushing responsibility for the hotel's own system failure onto the guest's corporate contact is inappropriate and does nothing to resolve the immediate checkout situation.
- [0] 今後は事前に法人アカウントのご確認をお願いいたします。
  - EN: In future, we ask that you confirm your corporate account in advance.
  - Feedback: Advising the guest on how to prevent the hotel's administrative error from affecting them again is condescending and entirely misplaces responsibility.

## Turn 3

**System:** You arrange for the guest to pay by personal card and promise a full refund within 3 business days via the corporate account; the guest is reassured by the written confirmation you provide.

**Guest:** OK. As long as I have something in writing I can send to our accounts department. Can you print that confirmation now?
**Guest JP:** わかりました。経理部に送れる書面があれば大丈夫です。今すぐその確認書を印刷してもらえますか？

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。ただいま印刷いたします。確認書には本日のお支払い内容、法人アカウントへの3営業日以内の全額返金の約束、担当マネージャーの署名を記載いたします。また、メールにてデジタル版もお送りします。ご出発前にご確認いただけますでしょうか。この度は多大なるご不便をおかけし、誠に申し訳ございませんでした。どうか安全なご出張をお過ごしくださいませ。
  - EN: Certainly — I am printing it right now. The confirmation will include the payment made today, the commitment to a full refund to your corporate account within three business days, and the duty manager's signature. I will also send you a digital copy by email. Please review it before you leave. I am truly sorry for the serious inconvenience this has caused. I hope you have a safe and successful trip.
  - Feedback: Describes exactly what the document contains including the signature, offers both printed and digital versions, invites the guest to review it before departure, and closes with a genuine apology and warm send-off — an ideal resolution.
- [1] 印刷いたします。支払い内容と返金の件を記載します。ご確認ください。
  - EN: I will print it. It will include the payment and refund details. Please check it.
  - Feedback: Produces the document correctly but does not mention the manager's signature, digital copy, or a warm closing.
- [0] 確認書は後ほどメールでお送りします。
  - EN: I will send the confirmation by email later.
  - Feedback: Offering to send the document after the guest has left for a flight — when they specifically need it now for their accounts department — does not meet the request and is unhelpful.
- [0] このような対応は通常行っておりませんので、ご了承ください。
  - EN: Please understand that this is not our usual procedure.
  - Feedback: Flagging that this is an unusual accommodation rather than simply providing it undermines the goodwill of the gesture and makes the guest feel like an inconvenience.
