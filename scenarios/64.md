id: 64
title: Locked Out of Room
description: Guest comes to the front desk saying they are locked out of their room.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** Hi, I've locked myself out — I left my key card inside the room.
**Guest JP:** すみません、部屋にカードキーを置いたまま出てしまって、入れなくなってしまいました。

### Choices

- [2] かしこまりました。大変ご不便をおかけいたしております。お部屋番号とお名前をお伺いできますでしょうか。確認次第、すぐに対応させていただきます。
  - EN: Certainly. I am sorry for the inconvenience. May I ask for your room number and name? As soon as I verify the details, I will take care of it right away.
  - Feedback: Warm acknowledgment of the inconvenience, immediately moves to verification — efficient and empathetic.
- [1] わかりました。お部屋の番号とお名前を教えていただけますか？
  - EN: Understood. Could you tell me your room number and name?
  - Feedback: Gets straight to the point, which is practical, but lacks an apology or acknowledgment of the guest's situation.
- [0] それはお客様の不注意です。
  - EN: That was careless of you.
  - Feedback: Blaming the guest is completely unacceptable — never imply fault when a guest is already in a difficult situation.
- [0] 少々お待ちください。担当に確認します。
  - EN: Please wait a moment. I will check with the person in charge.
  - Feedback: Passing the matter to someone else immediately, without first taking the guest's details, is unnecessarily slow.

## Turn 2

**System:** You politely verify the guest's identity by asking for their name and room number.

**Guest:** Sure — it's Tanaka, room 402.
**Guest JP:** はい、田中です。部屋は402号室です。

### Choices

- [2] 田中様、402号室でございますね。ただいまご本人確認が取れましたので、仮のカードキーをご用意いたします。少々お待ちくださいませ。
  - EN: Mr./Ms. Tanaka, room 402 — is that correct? I have now confirmed your identity, so I will prepare a temporary key card for you. Please bear with me for just a moment.
  - Feedback: Echoes the name and room number for confirmation, informs the guest of the next step, and closes with a polite request for patience.
- [1] はい、確認できました。カードキーをお作りします。
  - EN: Yes, I have confirmed that. I will make a key card for you.
  - Feedback: Functional and correct, but does not address the guest by name or fully echo the room number back.
- [0] パスポートなど身分証明書はお持ちですか？
  - EN: Do you have a passport or other ID?
  - Feedback: Demanding ID without first acknowledging the verbal confirmation can seem unnecessarily suspicious and unwelcoming.
- [0] しばらくお時間がかかります。
  - EN: It will take a while.
  - Feedback: Vague and unhelpful — the guest is locked out and needs reassurance, not an unexplained delay.

## Turn 3

**System:** You issue a temporary key card to the guest.

**Guest:** Thank you. Will my original card still work, or is it deactivated now?
**Guest JP:** ありがとうございます。元のカードはまだ使えますか？それとも使えなくなりましたか？

### Choices

- [2] こちらの仮カードをお渡しした時点で、元のカードは無効化されております。セキュリティ上のご安心のため、そのような仕組みになっております。ご不明な点がございましたら、何なりとお申し付けくださいませ。
  - EN: The original card has been deactivated as of the moment we issued this temporary card. This is done for your security and peace of mind. Please let us know if you have any other questions.
  - Feedback: Clear and complete — explains the reason behind the policy reassuringly and invites further questions.
- [1] 元のカードはもう使えません。このカードをお使いください。
  - EN: The original card no longer works. Please use this one.
  - Feedback: Correct information, but no explanation of why or any reassurance about security — slightly abrupt.
- [0] たぶん使えないと思います。
  - EN: I think it probably won't work.
  - Feedback: "Probably" is unacceptable here — staff must know the system and give a definitive answer.
- [0] 元のカードは捨ててください。
  - EN: Please throw away the original card.
  - Feedback: Issuing a disposal instruction without explanation sounds brusque, and the guest may want to handle their belongings themselves.
