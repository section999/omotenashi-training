---
id: 76
title: Guest Reports Missing Valuables
description: Guest calls the front desk to report that her necklace is missing from the room after housekeeping visited.
---

## Turn 1

**Guest:** I need to report something very serious. My necklace is missing from my room after your cleaning staff came in today.
**Guest JP:** 非常に深刻なことを報告しなければなりません。今日、清掃スタッフが来た後、部屋からネックレスがなくなっています。

### Choices

- [2] 大変申し訳ございません。ご不快な思いをおかけしてしまい、誠に恐縮でございます。このことを大変重くお受け止め、直ちに対応いたします。ただいまデューティーマネージャーをお部屋にお向かいさせますので、詳しいお話をお聞かせいただけますでしょうか。
  - EN: I sincerely apologise and I am truly sorry that you are experiencing this. We take this matter very seriously and will act immediately. I am sending the duty manager to your room right now — would you be willing to share the details with them?
  - Feedback: Apologises sincerely without making any premature judgments, signals the gravity of the situation, and escalates appropriately — the ideal opening to a sensitive report of missing valuables.
- [1] 大変失礼いたしました。状況を確認いたします。デューティーマネージャーをお送りします。
  - EN: I am very sorry. I will look into the situation and send the duty manager.
  - Feedback: Takes the correct action but lacks the empathetic depth and reassurance that is essential when a guest makes such a serious allegation.
- [0] 当ホテルのスタッフが盗るようなことはございません。
  - EN: Our staff would never take anything.
  - Feedback: Dismissing the guest's concern before any investigation has taken place is deeply inappropriate and will cause immediate escalation.
- [0] 貴重品はルームセーフにお預けいただくようご案内しております。
  - EN: We advise guests to place valuables in the in-room safe.
  - Feedback: Implying the guest is at fault for not using the safe before the facts are known is victim-blaming and entirely unacceptable.

## Turn 2

**System:** You take the report seriously, apologise, and dispatch the duty manager; the guest asks if this has happened before.

**Guest:** Has this kind of thing happened before at this hotel?
**Guest JP:** このホテルでこういうことが以前にもありましたか？

### Choices

- [2] お気持ちはよく存じます。ただ、過去の案件につきましては、お客様のプライバシーの観点からお答えすることが難しい状況でございます。今回のご報告は記録し、徹底的に調査いたします。デューティーマネージャーが間もなくお部屋にお伺いします。何卒ご安心いただきますよう、できる限りの対応をいたします。
  - EN: I completely understand why you ask. However, regarding past incidents, I am not in a position to discuss individual cases as a matter of privacy. What I can tell you is that your report is being recorded and will be fully investigated. The duty manager is on their way. Please be assured we will do everything possible.
  - Feedback: Answers honestly and appropriately — declines to discuss prior cases on privacy grounds rather than covering up, and refocuses on the immediate response — a balanced and professional answer.
- [1] 過去のことはお答えしかねますが、今回は必ず調査いたします。
  - EN: I am unable to comment on past cases, but this matter will definitely be investigated.
  - Feedback: Correct in content but lacks the fuller empathy and explanation that would reassure a justifiably anxious guest.
- [0] そのようなことは今まで一度もございません。
  - EN: Nothing like this has ever happened here before.
  - Feedback: Making an absolute claim about the hotel's history when under investigation is reckless and potentially dishonest — avoid categorical statements before facts are established.
- [0] 調査の結果、見つからなかった場合はご対応が難しくなります。
  - EN: If the necklace is not found in the investigation, it may be difficult for us to assist further.
  - Feedback: Signalling a limitation on responsibility before the investigation has even begun will deeply alarm the guest and appears defensive.

## Turn 3

**System:** The duty manager finds the necklace in the bathroom and calls the guest to report the good news; you must inform the guest and apologise again.

**Guest:** (Answers phone) Yes?
**Guest JP:** （電話に出る）はい？

### Choices

- [2] お待たせして大変申し訳ございませんでした。デューティーマネージャーよりご報告がございます。ネックレスはバスルームで見つかりました。ご心配をおかけしてしまいまして、誠に申し訳ございません。ただいまお部屋にお持ちします。
  - EN: I am so sorry to have kept you waiting and caused you such worry. I have good news — the duty manager has found your necklace in the bathroom. We sincerely apologise for the anxiety this has caused you. We will bring it to your room immediately.
  - Feedback: Leads with the good news clearly, apologises specifically for the distress caused, and confirms the immediate next step — a warm and complete resolution call.
- [1] ネックレスはバスルームで見つかりました。すぐにお持ちします。ご心配をおかけしました。
  - EN: Your necklace was found in the bathroom. We will bring it to you right away. I apologise for the worry this caused.
  - Feedback: Delivers the key information and apologises, but the ordering — news first, apology almost as an afterthought — could be warmer.
- [0] バスルームにありましたね。もしかしてご自身でそこに置かれましたか？
  - EN: It was in the bathroom. Perhaps you left it there yourself?
  - Feedback: Implying the guest was responsible for the scare after the distress they have experienced is unkind and will reopen the wound of the interaction.
- [0] 見つかりましたので、一件落着ということで。
  - EN: It has been found, so that is that settled.
  - Feedback: Treating a serious and distressing incident as simply "settled" with no apology shows a complete lack of empathy.
